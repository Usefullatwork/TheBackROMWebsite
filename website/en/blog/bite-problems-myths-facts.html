Bite Problems and Jaw Pain: Myths vs Facts

December 28, 2026 7 min read
  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Jaw Problems
  4. Bite Problems - Myths vs Facts

TL;DR - Bite Problems and TMD

  • Myth debunked: Research shows weak connection between bite problems and jaw pain
  • Adaptation: Adults have already adapted to their bite
  • Bigger factors: Stress, bruxism, posture affect more than bite alignment
  • Irreversible procedures: Bite adjustment, crowns, extensive dental work rarely necessary
  • Recommendation: Try conservative treatment first - avoid permanent procedures

The Old Theory

For decades there was a widespread belief that:

  • Perfect bite = healthy jaw
  • Bite problems = jaw pain
  • Correcting the bite = cure for TMD

This theory led to extensive dental treatments - grinding, crowns, bridge work - to "correct" the bite and thereby cure temporomandibular disorders.

But research has shown this to be wrong.

What Does the Research Say?

Systematic Reviews

Several large research reviews conclude that:

  • There is no strong connection between specific bite problems and TMD
  • Many people with a "bad" bite have no jaw problems
  • Many with a "perfect" bite have TMD
  • Bite correction provides no better results than conservative treatment

American Migraine Foundation Concludes

"Adults have already adapted to their bite, and only severe abnormalities contribute significantly to TMD."

Important Insight

The body is incredibly adaptable. Throughout life, musculature, ligaments, and the nervous system adapt to your bite. An "imperfect" bite that has worked for 30 years is probably not the cause of newly developed jaw problems.

Common Myths - And the Facts

Myth 1: "Overbite causes jaw pain"

Fact: Studies show no significant link between overbite and TMD. Many with large overbites have no problems, and many with TMD have normal bites.

Myth 2: "Crossbite causes jaw joint problems"

Fact: Although crossbite can affect chewing function, research shows it is not a strong predictor of TMD. Many people live perfectly fine with crossbite.

Myth 3: "Adult orthodontics prevents TMD"

Fact: There is no evidence that orthodontics prevents or treats TMD. In some cases, extensive orthodontic treatment can actually trigger temporary symptoms.

Myth 4: "Bite splint should correct the bite"

Fact: Bite splints are meant to protect teeth and relieve musculature - not to permanently change the bite. Splints claiming to "reposition the jaw" have no documented effect.

When Can Bite Alignment Actually Play a Role?

There are some exceptions where bite alignment can contribute to problems:

  • Acute changes: New filling or crown that is too high
  • Missing teeth: Leading to uneven loading
  • Severe malocclusions: Rare, extensive bite abnormalities
  • Recent extensive dental work: That has changed the bite

In these cases, adjustment may be sensible - but it's about acute changes, not congenital bite conditions.

What Actually Causes TMD?

Research points to other, more important factors:

Main Causes

  • Muscular tensions: Stress, teeth clenching, bruxism
  • Posture: Forward head position, neck tensions
  • Overload: Chewing tough food, gum, nail biting
  • Trauma: Impact to the jaw, whiplash
  • Sleep problems: Sleep apnea, restless sleep
  • Psychosocial factors: Anxiety, depression, stress

What Should You Do?

For New-Onset Jaw Pain

  1. Start with conservative treatment: Manual therapy, exercises, habit management
  2. Avoid irreversible procedures: Bite grinding, crowns, extensive dental work
  3. Consider lifestyle factors: Stress, sleep, posture
  4. Give it time: Most TMD cases improve with conservative treatment

If Someone Recommends Extensive Bite Correction

  • Ask about the evidence basis
  • Get a second opinion
  • Be skeptical of claims that a "perfect bite" will cure your problems
  • Always try reversible treatments first

Warning

Be careful with practitioners who recommend extensive, irreversible procedures such as bite grinding or complete dental rehabilitation for TMD. Such treatments have no documented effect and can in worst case worsen the problem.
