Chiropractor vs Physiotherapist: What's the Difference?

January 20, 2026 8 min read
  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Chiropractor vs Physiotherapist

TL;DR - Quick Summary

  • Education: Both have 5-6 years of university education
  • Chiropractor: Focus on joint manipulation and diagnostics, can order X-rays/MRI
  • Physiotherapist: Broader field, often focus on rehabilitation and exercises
  • Overlap: Many techniques are similar, both treat musculoskeletal conditions
  • Most important: Find a skilled practitioner you trust

Education and Authorization

Chiropractor

  • 5-6 years of university education (master's degree or equivalent)
  • Licensed healthcare professional in Norway
  • Primary contact - can receive patients without referral
  • Can order X-rays, MRI, CT and ultrasound
  • Can issue sick leave up to 12 weeks
  • Can refer to specialists

Physiotherapist

  • 3 years bachelor's degree + possible further education
  • Licensed healthcare professional in Norway
  • Primary contact - can receive patients without referral
  • Manual therapists have additional education and extended rights
  • Manual therapists can issue sick leave and order imaging

Treatment Techniques

Chiropractor typically uses:

  • Joint manipulation: Quick, precise adjustments of joints
  • Joint mobilization: Softer movements of joints
  • Soft tissue treatment: Massage and trigger point therapy
  • Needling: Dry needling and acupuncture
  • Exercises: Individually tailored home exercises
  • Shockwave therapy: For tendon conditions

Physiotherapist typically uses:

  • Exercise therapy: Often main focus
  • Manual treatment: Massage and mobilization
  • Electrotherapy: TENS, ultrasound, etc.
  • Rehabilitation: After injuries and surgeries
  • Specialized fields: Neurological, cardiopulmonary, pediatric physiotherapy

Manual therapists (physiotherapists with additional education) also use joint manipulation.

What Conditions Are Treated?

Both can treat:

  • Back and neck pain
  • Headaches and migraines
  • Shoulder, hip and knee problems
  • Sciatica and nerve root irritation
  • Muscle and tendon problems

Chiropractor often has special expertise in:

  • Acute back and neck conditions
  • Joint problems in the spine
  • Diagnostic assessment

Physiotherapist often has special expertise in:

  • Rehabilitation after surgeries
  • Long-term exercise programs
  • Neurological conditions
  • Heart and lung diseases
  • Pediatric physiotherapy

Practical Differences

Aspect Chiropractor Physiotherapist
First consultation 30-60 minutes 30-60 minutes
Follow-up 15-30 minutes 30-60 minutes
Referral needed No No
Can order imaging Yes Only manual therapists
Can issue sick leave Yes (up to 12 weeks) Only manual therapists
HELFO reimbursement Yes Yes (with operating subsidy)

What Does the Research Say?

Research shows that both chiropractic treatment and physiotherapy are effective for musculoskeletal conditions. For most conditions, there is little difference in outcomes between the two professions.

The most important factors are often:

  • That you get the correct diagnosis
  • That the treatment is evidence-based
  • That you get good advice and exercises
  • That you trust your practitioner

When to Choose a Chiropractor?

  • For acute back or neck conditions
  • When you want rapid diagnostic clarification
  • For joint-related problems
  • When you need a referral for MRI/X-ray
  • When you need sick leave

When to Choose a Physiotherapist?

  • For longer rehabilitation programs
  • After surgeries
  • For neurological conditions
  • When the main focus is exercise and training
  • For heart/lung problems

Can You See Both?

Absolutely! Many patients combine treatment from both a chiropractor and physiotherapist. For example, a chiropractor can help with acute joint problems, while a physiotherapist follows up with longer-term exercise programs.

The Most Important Thing

The most important thing is not what title the practitioner has, but that you find a skilled and evidence-based practitioner who takes the time to examine you thoroughly and create a good treatment plan.

Feel free to ask about:

  • The practitioner's experience with your type of condition
  • What you can expect from the treatment
  • How many treatments are typically needed
  • What you can do yourself between treatments
