Dizziness - Causes, Symptoms and Effective Treatment

A comprehensive guide to understanding and managing dizziness, based on current research and clinical experience.
January 15, 2025 Mads Finstad Clinical

Dizziness is one of the most challenging conditions patients can experience. It can come suddenly and be so intense that it disrupts daily activities, work and quality of life. As a chiropractor, I meet many patients who have lived with dizziness for a long time without receiving satisfactory help.

This article is based on the latest research and clinical guidelines (2024-2025) and provides you with a deep understanding of the complex nature of dizziness. You will learn about the most common causes, how your body maintains balance, and which effective treatment methods are available.

The Body's Balance System - A Finely Tuned Interplay

To understand dizziness, you must first look at our amazing balance system. Balance is not one single thing, but a continuous collaboration between three critical systems that constantly communicate with the brain:

The Three Balance Sensors:

  1. The Inner Ear (Vestibular System) - Our built-in gyroscope with semicircular canals that register head movements and otolith organs that sense gravity
  2. The Eyes (Visual System) - Provides information about where I am in relation to my surroundings
  3. Muscles and Joints (Proprioceptive System) - Especially important in the neck, where sensors tell the brain about the head's position

When one of these systems fails, an "information chaos" occurs in the brain that results in dizziness. It's like receiving conflicting navigation signals - the brain no longer knows what's up and down.

Common Causes of Dizziness

1. BPPV (Benign Paroxysmal Positional Vertigo) - The Most Treatable Form

BPPV is the most common cause of dizziness and accounts for 20-30% of all cases. The condition occurs when small calcium crystals (otoliths) dislodge from their normal place in the inner ear and end up in the semicircular canals.

Characteristic Symptoms:

  • Brief, intense spinning episodes (10-60 seconds)
  • Triggered by specific head movements (turning in bed, looking up)
  • Nausea and involuntary eye movements (nystagmus)
  • Symptoms come and go, can last for months

Treatment: The Epley maneuver has a success rate of 80-90% and can provide immediate improvement. This is a series of controlled head movements that move the crystals back to the right position.

2. Neck-Related Dizziness - Often Overlooked

This form of dizziness originates from problems in the upper neck and is very common but often misdiagnosed. The neck contains critical sensors that tell the brain about the head's position.

Typical Signs:

  • Unsteadiness and swaying feeling (nautical vertigo)
  • Symptoms worsen with neck positions or movements
  • Often accompanied by headache and neck pain
  • No hearing loss or tinnitus

3. Meniere's Disease - A Chronic Condition

Meniere's disease is a chronic disorder of the inner ear caused by increased fluid pressure (endolymphatic hydrops). The condition causes classic attacks that include all four main symptoms.

Four Main Characteristics:

  • Severe rotatory vertigo (20 minutes to several hours)
  • Fluctuating hearing loss (low frequency)
  • Tinnitus (ringing in the ears)
  • Pressure sensation in the affected ear

4. Vestibular Neuritis - Acute Balance Disturbance

This is an inflammation of the balance nerve, usually after a viral infection. The condition causes sudden, severe dizziness that lasts for several days to weeks.

Characteristics:

  • Constant, intense carousel feeling
  • Severe nausea and vomiting
  • Significant balance problems
  • No hearing loss (distinguishes it from labyrinthitis)

Diagnosis - What Can You Expect?

A Good Conversation Is Key

A thorough examination always starts with listening to your history. I map when symptoms occur, what triggers them, how long they last, and if there are other accompanying symptoms.

Specific Tests

  • Dix-Hallpike test - Gold standard for detecting BPPV
  • Neck examination - Mobility, stiffness and muscle tension
  • Eye movement tests - Reveal nystagmus and vestibular problems
  • Balance and gait tests - General coordination and stability

Treatment Options

Treatment Tailored to the Cause

BPPV

Repositioning maneuvers (Epley/Semont) - 80-90% success rate

Neck-Related Dizziness

Manual treatment, joint corrections and specific exercises

Vestibular Neuritis

Vestibular rehabilitation and compensation training

Meniere's Disease

Lifestyle changes, medications and specialist follow-up

Vestibular Rehabilitation - The Cornerstone of Treatment

Vestibular rehabilitation is specialized physical therapy for the balance system with documented effect. Treatment includes:

  • Gaze stabilization - Exercises to maintain focus during head movements
  • Balance exercises - Progressive training on challenging surfaces
  • Habituation - Exposure to movements that trigger symptoms

When Should You Seek Help?

Call emergency services immediately if:

  • Severe headache (worst ever)
  • Speech difficulties or facial paralysis
  • Vision problems or numbness
  • Chest pain together with dizziness

Contact a doctor if:

  • Dizziness lasts more than 3 days
  • You have hearing problems or tinnitus
  • Symptoms affect daily function
  • Increasing frequency of attacks

Myths and Facts About Dizziness

Myth

"Dizziness is just part of getting older"

Fact

"Most forms of dizziness can be treated effectively regardless of age"

Myth

"You should lie still when you're dizzy"

Fact

"Adapted movement and balance training is crucial for recovery"

Summary

Dizziness is a complex but treatable condition. With proper diagnosis and targeted treatment, most people can experience significant improvement and return to normal activity. The most important thing is not to ignore the symptoms, but to seek professional help to identify the underlying cause.

Remember that you don't have to live with dizziness. There are effective treatment methods, and many of my patients experience dramatic improvement already after the first consultation.
