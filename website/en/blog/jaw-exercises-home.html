Jaw Exercises at Home: Complete Exercise Program

December 28, 2026 10 min read
  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Jaw Problems
  4. Jaw Exercises

TL;DR - What You Need to Know

  • Frequency: 2-3 times daily, consistency is more important than duration
  • Duration: 5-10 minutes per session
  • Progression: Start gently, increase gradually
  • Goal: Relaxation before movement, movement before strength
  • Important: Pain during exercises means you need to reduce intensity

Why Are Exercises Important?

Exercises are a cornerstone of TMD treatment and have documented effect:

  • Reduces muscle tension and pain
  • Improves mobility in the temporomandibular joint
  • Breaks the vicious cycle of pain and tension
  • Gives you control over your own complaints

Studies show that exercises can be as effective as other treatments for many jaw problems.

Before You Start

  • Check your resting position: Lips together, teeth apart, tongue against the palate
  • Warm up: Gentle massage or heat before exercises
  • Avoid: Exercises that cause significant pain increase
  • Be patient: It takes time to see results (weeks, not days)

Phase 1: Relaxation (Start Here)

1. Breathing and Jaw Relaxation

  1. Sit or lie comfortably
  2. Close your eyes and breathe slowly through your nose
  3. On exhale: Let the jaw "fall" down slightly (lips still closed)
  4. Feel the jaw muscles relax more with each exhale
  5. 5-10 breaths, repeat several times daily

2. Resting Position Training

  1. Say the letter "N" softly
  2. Notice where your tongue is - against the palate behind the front teeth
  3. Let the teeth be separated by 2-3 mm
  4. Lips relaxed together
  5. This is the jaw's ideal resting position

Tip: Set phone alarms to check your jaw position throughout the day.

3. Progressive Muscle Relaxation for the Jaw

  1. Clench your teeth lightly for 5 seconds
  2. Relax completely for 10 seconds
  3. Notice the difference between tension and relaxation
  4. Repeat 5 times

NB: Don't use maximum strength - only light tension!

Phase 2: Mobility

4. Controlled Opening

  1. Place your tongue against the palate
  2. Open your mouth as far as you can while keeping your tongue up
  3. This limits how wide you open and trains controlled movement
  4. Hold for 5 seconds, then close slowly
  5. 10 repetitions

5. Side Movement

  1. Open your mouth slightly (1-2 cm)
  2. Move your lower jaw slowly to one side
  3. Hold for 2-3 seconds
  4. Move to the other side
  5. 10 repetitions to each side

6. Forward Movement

  1. Open your mouth slightly
  2. Push your lower jaw forward (like a bulldog)
  3. Hold for 2-3 seconds
  4. Pull back slowly
  5. 10 repetitions

Phase 3: Light Strengthening

7. Resistance Against Opening

  1. Place 2 fingers under your chin
  2. Open your mouth while giving gentle resistance with your fingers
  3. Hold for 5 seconds
  4. Relax
  5. 10 repetitions

8. Resistance Against Closing

  1. Place two fingers on your lower front teeth
  2. Try to close your mouth while giving gentle resistance
  3. Hold for 5 seconds
  4. Relax
  5. 10 repetitions

9. Resistance to the Side

  1. Place your thumb against the outside of your jaw on one side
  2. Push your jaw toward your thumb while you give resistance
  3. Hold for 5 seconds
  4. Switch sides
  5. 10 repetitions each side

Self-Massage Techniques

Masseter Muscle (Chewing Muscle)

  1. Place your fingers on your cheeks, in front of your ears
  2. Clench your teeth lightly - feel the muscle tense
  3. Massage with circular movements for 30-60 seconds
  4. Can be slightly tender, but shouldn't be painful

Temporalis (Temple Muscle)

  1. Place your fingertips on your temples
  2. Massage with circular movements
  3. Continue up toward the hairline
  4. 30-60 seconds

Sample Program

Beginner Program (First 2 Weeks)

3 times daily:

  • Breathing relaxation (1 min)
  • Resting position check
  • Controlled opening (10 reps)
  • Side movement (10 reps)
  • Total: 5 minutes

Intermediate Program (Week 3-6)

2-3 times daily:

  • All beginner exercises
  • Add resistance exercises (10 reps each)
  • Self-massage (2 min)
  • Total: 10 minutes

Maintenance Program

1-2 times daily:

  • Relaxation exercises
  • Favorite exercises that work for you
  • Total: 5 minutes

Common Mistakes

  • Doing too much: Overtraining can worsen pain
  • Too strong: Exercises should be gentle
  • Skipping relaxation: Relaxation is just as important as movement
  • Inconsistency: Regular short sessions beat sporadic long sessions
  • Ignoring pain: Pain is a signal to reduce intensity

When to Seek Help

  • Exercises worsen pain
  • You notice clicking or locking getting worse
  • No improvement after 4 weeks of regular exercises
  • Unsure if you're doing the exercises correctly
