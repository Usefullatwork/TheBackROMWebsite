Jaw Pain During Pregnancy: Why It Happens and What You Can Do

December 28, 2026 7 min read
  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. TMJ Disorders
  4. Pregnancy and Jaw Pain

TL;DR - Pregnancy and Jaw

  • Hormones: Relaxin and estrogen affect ligaments - including in the jaw joint
  • Stress: Increased worry can lead to more teeth grinding
  • Posture: Changed center of gravity affects neck and jaw
  • Treatment: Manual therapy is safe throughout pregnancy
  • Self-help: Exercises, heat, relaxation - no medication needed

Why Can Pregnancy Cause Jaw Pain?

Several factors during pregnancy can affect the jaw joint:

1. Hormonal Changes

During pregnancy, the body produces increased amounts of the hormone relaxin. This hormone loosens ligaments to prepare the body for childbirth - but it affects all joints, including the jaw joint:

  • Increased joint mobility can cause instability
  • Ligaments in the jaw become looser
  • Increased risk of clicking and discomfort

Estrogen also affects pain perception and can make the jaw joint more sensitive.

2. Stress and Worry

Pregnancy is a time with many thoughts and worries:

  • Increased stress levels often lead to teeth grinding
  • Sleep problems worsen bruxism
  • Jaw tension as a response to uncertainty

3. Postural Changes

As the belly grows:

  • Center of gravity shifts forward
  • Many compensate with increased lower back curve
  • Shoulders pull forward, neck extends
  • Forward head position strains the jaw joint

4. Sleeping Position

  • Side lying (recommended in last trimester) can press on the jaw
  • Restless sleep increases nighttime grinding
  • Frequent waking disrupts the sleep cycle

Common Symptoms

  • Jaw pain, often worse in the morning
  • Increased teeth grinding (partner hears it)
  • Headache in the temples
  • Clicking in the jaw joint
  • Stiff and tender jaw muscles
  • Pain when chewing

Safe Treatment During Pregnancy

Manual Treatment

Chiropractic treatment is safe throughout pregnancy:

  • Soft tissue treatment: Gentle massage of jaw muscles
  • Mobilization: Gentle techniques for jaw joint
  • Neck treatment: Relieves referred pain
  • Adaptation: Treatment is adjusted according to trimester

Safe for Mother and Baby

Manual treatment of jaw and neck does not negatively affect pregnancy. I avoid high-risk techniques and adapt treatment to your situation.

Self-Help - Without Medication

Many want to avoid medication during pregnancy. Here are effective alternatives:

Heat Treatment

  • Warm cloth or heat pack on jaw 10-15 min
  • Loosens tight muscles
  • Completely safe during pregnancy

Exercises

  • Controlled opening: 10 reps, 3 times daily
  • Side movements: Careful, pain-free
  • N-technique: Rest position for the jaw
  • Neck stretching: Light stretch to the sides

Relaxation

  • Breathing techniques before bedtime
  • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • Mindfulness or meditation

Sleep Tips

  • Use pregnancy pillow for support
  • Avoid placing hand under cheek
  • Relaxation routine before bedtime

What About Bite Splint?

If you already use a bite splint, you can continue with it. Be aware that:

  • Hormonal changes can affect gums - the splint may feel different
  • Nausea in first trimester may make it uncomfortable to use splint
  • Talk to your dentist if the splint no longer fits well

When Should You Seek Help?

  • Persistent jaw pain not responding to self-help
  • Worsening headache
  • Locking in the jaw joint
  • Significant impact on sleep or eating

Red Flags - Contact Doctor

  • Intense headache with visual disturbances (may be preeclampsia)
  • Swelling in face/jaw that increases rapidly
  • Fever with jaw pain
  • Numbness in face or lips

After Birth

Many experience improvement after birth when hormones normalize. But:

  • Breastfeeding can maintain some hormonal influence
  • Sleep deprivation with newborn increases stress levels
  • Breastfeeding positions can affect neck and posture

Continue with exercises and self-help, and seek treatment if symptoms persist.
