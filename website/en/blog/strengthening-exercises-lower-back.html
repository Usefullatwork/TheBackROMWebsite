Strengthening Exercises for Lower Back: Build a Stronger Back

January 20, 2026 12 min read
  1. Home
  2. Blog
  3. Lower Back
  4. Strengthening exercises lower back

TL;DR - Summary

  • Core muscles: Abs, back, hips, and pelvic floor work together
  • Research: Strength training reduces recurrence risk by up to 50%
  • Frequency: 2-3 times per week gives good results
  • Progression: Start simple, increase gradually
  • Consistency: Regular training over time is the key

Why Strength Training Helps

Strong core muscles give the spine better support and stability. Research shows that people with weak core muscles have higher risk of lower back pain.

Strength training works through several mechanisms:

  • Increased stability in the spine
  • Better movement control
  • Reduced load on discs and joints
  • Improved blood supply to tissues
  • Pain-relieving effect

The Important Muscle Groups

Anterior Core (Front)

  • Rectus abdominis: The "six-pack" muscle
  • Transversus abdominis: Deep stabilizer, like a natural belt
  • Obliques: Diagonal abdominal muscles for rotation

Posterior Core (Back)

  • Erector spinae: The long back extensors
  • Multifidi: Small, deep stabilizers between vertebrae
  • Quadratus lumborum: Side stabilizer

Hips and Pelvis

  • Gluteus maximus/medius: The glutes
  • Iliopsoas: Hip flexor
  • Pelvic floor: Base for the entire core

Basic Exercises (Start Here)

1. Dead Bug

Target: Deep core stability

  1. Lie on your back with arms up and legs at 90 degrees
  2. Press lower back flat against the floor
  3. Slowly lower opposite arm and leg toward the floor
  4. Keep lower back stable the entire time
  5. Return to start, switch sides
  6. 10-15 repetitions each side

2. Bird-Dog

Target: Back extensors and stability

  1. Get on all fours, neutral spine
  2. Extend opposite arm and leg horizontally
  3. Hold for 3-5 seconds
  4. Switch sides without swaying in the back
  5. 10-12 repetitions each side

3. Glute Bridge

Target: Glutes and hip extensors

  1. Lie on your back with bent knees, feet on the floor
  2. Squeeze glutes and lift hips
  3. Create a straight line from shoulders to knees
  4. Hold for 2-3 seconds
  5. Lower down with control
  6. 15-20 repetitions

4. Plank

Target: Total core stability

  1. Support on forearms and toes
  2. Body straight like a plank
  3. Tighten abs and glutes
  4. Hold as long as you can with good technique
  5. Goal: 30-60 seconds

5. Side Plank

Target: Obliques and side stability

  1. Lie on your side, support on forearm
  2. Lift hips up from the floor
  3. Keep body in a straight line
  4. Hold 20-30 seconds
  5. Switch sides

Intermediate Exercises

6. Pallof Press

Target: Anti-rotation stability

  1. Stand sideways to a cable machine or resistance band
  2. Hold the cable/band in front of your chest
  3. Press straight out and hold 2-3 seconds
  4. Resist rotation in upper body
  5. 10-12 repetitions each side

7. Romanian Deadlift (Light Weight)

Target: Back of thighs and back

  1. Stand with light dumbbells or barbell
  2. Hinge from hips with almost straight legs
  3. Keep spine neutral
  4. Lower until you feel stretch in hamstrings
  5. Extend hips to come up
  6. 8-12 repetitions

8. Goblet Squat

Target: Functional strength, thighs, and glutes

  1. Hold a kettlebell or dumbbell in front of chest
  2. Feet shoulder-width apart
  3. Sit down until thighs are parallel to floor
  4. Keep spine neutral
  5. Push up through heels
  6. 10-15 repetitions

Weekly Program

Beginner Level (First 4 Weeks)

2-3 times per week:

  • Dead bug: 2 x 10 each side
  • Bird-dog: 2 x 10 each side
  • Glute bridge: 2 x 15
  • Plank: 2 x 20-30 sec
  • Side plank: 2 x 15-20 sec each side

Intermediate Level (Weeks 5-8)

  • Dead bug: 3 x 12 each side
  • Bird-dog with hold: 3 x 10 (5 sec hold)
  • Single-leg glute bridge: 3 x 10 each side
  • Plank: 3 x 45 sec
  • Side plank: 3 x 30 sec each side
  • Pallof press: 2 x 10 each side

Advanced Level

Add:

  • Romanian deadlift: 3 x 10
  • Goblet squat: 3 x 12
  • Plank with arm/leg lift: 3 x 30 sec
  • Ab wheel rollout (if tolerated)

Important Tips

  • Quality over quantity: Good technique is more important than many repetitions
  • Breathing: Don't hold your breath - exhale on exertion
  • Progression: Increase gradually - don't skip levels
  • Pain: Mild discomfort is okay, sharp pain is not
  • Consistency: Regularity beats intensity

When You Shouldn't Train

Be careful with acute conditions like lumbago, sciatica, or disc herniation. Talk to a professional before starting training.

  • With acute, severe pain
  • With numbness or weakness in legs
  • With fever or illness
  • Right after an acute injury
