Stress Neck and Trigger Points

January 2026 Mads Finstad 12 min read

TL;DR - Quick Summary

  • What it is: Chronic muscle tension in neck and shoulders triggered by psychological stress
  • Why trapezius: EMG studies show trapezius is automatically activated during mental load
  • How common: 42-63% annual prevalence of neck pain among office workers
  • Treatment: Combination of manual therapy, strength training and stress management

Why Does Stress Settle in the Neck?

Have you noticed that you raise your shoulders when stressed? It's not coincidental. The upper trapezius - the large muscle running from the neck out to the shoulders - is one of the body's most stress-sensitive muscles.

That the expression "pain in the neck" has become synonymous with irritation and stress is no coincidence. Modern work life - with long days in front of screens, tight deadlines and constant availability - creates perfect conditions for what many call "stress neck".

What Actually Happens in the Muscle During Stress?

When you experience stress, the brain sends signals that activate the musculature. This is part of the body's "fight or flight" response. The problem is that modern stressors rarely require physical action - but the muscle tension remains.

EMG Studies Document the Connection

Electromyography (EMG) measures electrical activity in muscles. Studies have consistently shown that the trapezius muscle is activated during mental load, even without physical work:

  • Lundberg and colleagues (1994) found that mental stress tests gave significant increase in trapezius EMG activity
  • 70% of those with neck pain had higher EMG activity at work
  • During stress, trapezius gets fewer rest breaks

How Common Is Stress Neck?

  • Annual prevalence among office workers: 42-63% report neck pain
  • Trigger points: 93% of patients with chronic neck pain have trigger points in upper trapezius
  • Latent trigger points: 78.8% of healthy people have latent (sleeping) trigger points in upper trapezius

Women are affected more often than men - nearly double the risk of neck pain in women.

Symptoms of Stress Neck

  • Tightness and tension: Feeling that shoulders are "by the ears"
  • Tender "knots": Trigger points that are painful under pressure
  • Referred pain: Pain radiating from trigger points toward the back of head, temple and behind the eye
  • Headache: 80% of patients with chronic tension headache have active trigger points in upper trapezius
  • Reduced mobility: Difficulty turning head or looking up
  • Worsening with stress: Symptoms typically increase during intense periods

Treatment

What Can the Chiropractor Do?

A network meta-analysis from 2024 shows that manual therapy provides short-term improvement in pain, pressure sensitivity and function for trigger points. Treatment includes:

  • Ischemic compression: Sustained pressure on the trigger point
  • Dry needling: Thin needles in the trigger point
  • Mobilization: Soft tissue treatment and joint movement
  • Exercise-based rehabilitation: The most important thing for lasting improvement

What Can You Do Yourself?

Strength training: Neck/shoulder strength exercises 2-3 times per week have moderate to good evidence for pain relief.

Stretching: Hold each stretch for 30 seconds - this is optimal duration according to research.

Breathing techniques: Diaphragmatic breathing (belly breathing) activates the vagus nerve which dampens the stress response.

Ergonomics: Screen at eye level, forearms supported, regular breaks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can stress really cause physical pain?

Yes. EMG studies clearly show that mental stress increases muscle activity in trapezius. There's no contradiction between "psychological" stress and "physical" pain - the body reacts to both.

Does massage help?

Massage and manual therapy can provide good short-term effect. But for lasting improvement, you should combine treatment with strength training and stress management.

Should I stop exercising when I'm in pain?

No, generally not. Moderate activity is usually better than rest for muscular problems.
