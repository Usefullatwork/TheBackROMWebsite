Hip Pain Diagnosis Guide

Where does it hurt? Use this guide to find the likely cause.

Chiropractor Mads Finstad, Klinikk for alle Majorstua

Hip pain can be confusing. Pain on the outside is often called "bursitis" (but rarely is). Pain in the buttock is often confused with back trouble. This guide helps you navigate the symptoms based on the most important clue: Location.

1

Pain on the OUTSIDE (Lateral Hip)

Condition Key Symptoms Tests & Differentiation
GTPS (Gluteal Tendinopathy) Very Common Pain lying on side at night.
Pain with stairs, walking uphill.
Tender to touch over the bony prominence (trochanter). 		Single Leg Stance: Standing on one leg for 30s reproduces the familiar pain.
Read more about GTPS →
Referred Back Pain Common Pain radiates from low back.
Often goes past the knee.
Variable pain pattern (good days/bad days). 		Slump Test: Stretching the nerve path reproduces pain.
Pain often triggered by bending back (not hip).
Back vs Hip guide →
Trochanteric Bursitis Rare (<10%)< /span> Acute swelling, redness, and warmth.
Pain with ANY movement of the leg. 		Most "bursitis" diagnoses are actually tendon injuries (GTPS). True bursitis shows signs of inflammation.
Why it's rarely bursitis →
2

Pain in the GROIN (Anterior Hip)

Condition Key Symptoms Tests & Differentiation
Hip Osteoarthritis (OA) Common (50+) Morning stiffness.
Deep ache in groin/front thigh.
Difficulty putting on socks. 		Restricted Range: Loss of internal rotation.
X-rays show joint space narrowing.
Read about Hip OA →
FAI (Impingement) Young/Active Sharp pinching with deep squats/flexion.
Clicking or catching.
"C-Sign" (gripping hip with hand). 		FADIR Test: Deep flexion + internal rotation causes sharp pain.
Good range of motion otherwise.
Read about FAI →
Athletic Pubalgia Athletes Sharp pain with twisting/sprinting.
Pain localized to pubic bone. 		Pain with resisted sit-ups or squeezing legs together.
Common in soccer/hockey.
Sports Hernia guide →
Iliopsoas Tendinopathy Runners/Dancers Pain when lifting the knee (marching).
Snapping sound in front of hip. 		Pain with resisted hip flexion.
Tender deep in the abdomen/groin.
Iliopsoas guide →
3

Pain in the BUTTOCK (Posterior Hip)

Condition Key Symptoms Tests & Differentiation
Sciatica (Back Issue) Common Electric/shooting pain.
Radiates past the knee to foot.
Numbness in toes/foot. 		Straight Leg Raise: Reproduces leg pain.
Often worse with bending forward (slumping).
Sciatica vs Hip →
Piriformis Syndrome Uncommon Deep ache in buttock.
Worse with sitting (wallet in pocket).
Radiates down thigh but stops at knee. 		FAIR Test: Stretching piriformis (knee to opposite shoulder) causes pain.
No back stiffness.
Piriformis guide →
SI Joint Dysfunction Common Sharp pain at the "dimple" (PSIS).
Pain turning in bed.
Pain standing on one leg. 		Pointing Test: Patient points to the specific "dimple" with one finger.
Pain rarely goes below the knee.
SI Joint guide →

Still unsure?

Self-diagnosis is a good start, but a thorough clinical examination is the only way to be 100% sure. At Klinikk for alle Majorstua, we perform ortopedic and neurological tests to pinpoint exactly where your pain comes from.
