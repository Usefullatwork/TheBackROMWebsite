Red Flags for Back Pain

When to seek emergency help - and when it's safe to wait | Chiropractor Mads Finstad, Majorstua

Red flags are warning signs that may indicate back pain is caused by a serious underlying condition requiring urgent medical evaluation.

The good news is that serious pathology is rare. A large Australian study found that less than 1% of patients with back pain in primary care have serious illness requiring medical referral. In a review of over 6,600 patients in general practice, only 21 tumors were identified, equivalent to 0.3%.

Quick Summary

  • Over 99% of back pain is harmless
  • Serious conditions: Cauda equina syndrome, spinal infection, vertebral fracture, spinal malignancy
  • Call emergency services for: Bladder dysfunction, groin numbness, bilateral leg weakness
  • Urgent care for: Back pain with fever, unexplained weight loss

When should you seek help immediately?

For numbness in the groin, problems with urination/bowel control, or increasing weakness in both legs - call emergency services or go to the ER.

1

Cauda Equina Syndrome - Emergency Situation

Cauda equina syndrome (CES) occurs when the nerve bundle at the bottom of the spinal cord becomes compressed, usually by a large disc herniation. The condition affects approximately 1.6 per 100,000 per year - rare, but very serious.

1.6 per 100,000 per year
48h critical time window
84% have bladder symptoms

Symptoms to Watch For

Cauda Equina Symptoms

  • Bladder dysfunction (84%): Difficulty starting urination, weak stream, feeling of incomplete emptying, or urinary incontinence
  • Saddle anesthesia (71%): Numbness or altered sensation around the rectum, genitals, and inner thighs
  • Bowel dysfunction (62%): Altered bowel control
  • Bilateral leg weakness: Weakness in both legs that develops rapidly

Why is it urgent?

Deterioration can occur at approximately 1% per hour. Delay beyond 48 hours was associated with 9.6 times increased mortality. There is no "safe" waiting time.

2

Spinal Infection

Spinal infections include spondylodiscitis and epidural abscess. The incidence has increased significantly - from 5.4 to 11.0 per 100,000 between 2005 and 2021.

Risk Factors

  • Diabetes (18-33%)
  • Intravenous drug use (9-19%)
  • Immunosuppression
  • Recent spinal surgery

Important to Know

Fever is only present in about 55% of patients. The absence of fever therefore does not rule out infection.

3

Vertebral Fracture

Compression fractures in the spine particularly affect the elderly. Approximately 25% of women over 50 have at least one vertebral fracture, and the incidence increases to 40-50% in people over 80.

Risk Factors

  • Osteoporosis
  • Long-term steroid use
  • Age over 70
  • Previous vertebral fracture
4

Spinal Malignancy

Cancer as a cause of back pain is rare in primary care - less than 1% of back pain patients. Approximately 90% of spinal tumors are metastases from cancer elsewhere.

Strongest Risk Factor

Previous cancer diagnosis gives a likelihood ratio of 23.7 - this is the single red flag with the highest diagnostic value.

5

When You Must Act Quickly

CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES IMMEDIATELY for:

  • Sudden urinary retention or incontinence
  • Numbness in the groin/saddle area
  • Bilateral weakness or numbness in both legs
  • Rapidly progressive paralysis
  • Loss of bowel control

When in doubt, call - better one time too many than one time too few.

CONTACT URGENT CARE for:

  • Back pain combined with fever
  • Severe back pain with new neurological symptoms
  • Acute worsening that doesn't respond to regular painkillers

BOOK A GP APPOINTMENT for:

  • Persistent back pain that doesn't improve after 4-6 weeks
  • Unexplained weight loss combined with back pain
  • Constant pain that isn't relieved by rest
  • Night pain that wakes you regularly
  • Back pain in someone with a previous cancer diagnosis
6

The Chiropractor's Role with Red Flags

In Norway, chiropractors have been primary contact practitioners with independent treatment and diagnostic authority since 2006. You can see a chiropractor directly without a referral from a doctor.

What the Chiropractor Does

  • Systematic screening for red flags as part of the initial examination
  • Assessment of symptoms of cauda equina syndrome, infection, fracture, and malignancy
  • Referral to specialist, hospital, or imaging when serious pathology is suspected
  • Treatment of the 99%+ who have harmless back pain

Chiropractors in Norway have referral rights to MRI, CT, X-ray, and ultrasound, as well as the right to issue sick leave for up to 12 weeks.

7

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my back pain is dangerous?
Most back pain is harmless. Watch for combinations of symptoms: Back pain alone without other symptoms is rarely serious. It's when back pain is combined with bladder dysfunction, groin numbness, unexplained weight loss, fever, or rapidly progressive weakness that you should seek prompt help.
Can a chiropractor detect serious illness?
Yes. Norwegian chiropractors are licensed healthcare professionals with 5-6 years of education. A thorough clinical examination with neurological testing is often more informative than imaging. When serious pathology is suspected, the chiropractor has a duty to refer further.
How quickly does cauda equina syndrome develop?
It varies. Some experience gradual worsening over days to weeks, others get sudden symptoms. If you have established back pain and develop new symptoms like bladder dysfunction or groin numbness, you should seek help immediately.
