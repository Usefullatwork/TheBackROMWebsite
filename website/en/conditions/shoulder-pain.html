Shoulder Pain - Complete Guide

Everything you need to know about causes, symptoms and treatment | Chiropractor Mads Finstad, Oslo

The shoulder is the body's most mobile joint. You can lift your arm overhead, rotate it backward, and reach in almost any direction. This flexibility comes at a price - the shoulder is also more susceptible to strain and injury than other joints.

Approximately 16% of adults experience shoulder pain during a year (Lucas et al., 2022). This makes shoulder problems the third most common musculoskeletal condition after low back and knee problems. Women are affected slightly more often than men, and the incidence increases from age 40.

The good news? Most shoulder pain improves with proper treatment. Research shows that 60-85% achieve good improvement without surgery when they receive appropriate care.

Quick Summary

  • Prevalence: About 16% of adults experience shoulder pain during a year
  • Most common causes: Rotator cuff-related pain (about 70-85%), frozen shoulder, referred from neck
  • Treatment: Manual treatment combined with exercises - most improve without surgery
  • Timeline: Mild problems 4-6 weeks, moderate 6-12 weeks, frozen shoulder 12-24 months
  • Red flags: Severe pain after fall, fever, numbness in arm, visible deformity
  • No referral needed: You can come directly to a chiropractor - no referral necessary

Rotator Cuff-Related Shoulder Pain (RCRSP)

The most common cause - about 70-85% of all shoulder pain

RCRSP (Rotator Cuff-Related Shoulder Pain) is the modern umbrella term that replaces the outdated "impingement syndrome." The rotator cuff consists of four small muscles that keep the upper arm bone stable in the socket.

Why was the name changed?

Research has shown that the old "pinching theory" - that tendons get pinched under the shoulder's roof - isn't accurate. Large surgical studies from 2018 (CSAW, FIMPACT) showed that surgery to remove bone spurs didn't give better results than placebo surgery.

Under the RCRSP umbrella you'll find:

  • Rotator cuff tendinopathy (tendon inflammation) - most common in supraspinatus
  • Subacromial bursitis (bursa inflammation)
  • Partial and complete tendon tears
  • Calcific tendinitis (calcium deposits in tendon)

Typical Symptoms

  • Pain when lifting the arm, especially to the side or overhead
  • "Painful arc" - pain between 60-120 degrees of lift
  • Aching night pain that disturbs sleep
  • Feeling of weakness in the arm

Prognosis: 73-80% achieve good improvement with structured exercises and manual treatment. Surgery is rarely necessary.

2

Frozen Shoulder (Adhesive Capsulitis)

Frozen shoulder is a condition where the joint capsule around the shoulder becomes thick, inflamed and tight. This leads to gradually increasing stiffness and pain. The condition goes through three characteristic phases:

Freezing Phase (2-9 months)

Gradually increasing pain and stiffness. Night pain can be intense.

Frozen Phase (4-12 months)

Pain decreases somewhat, but stiffness is at its worst. Daily activities become difficult.

Thawing Phase (6-24 months)

Movement gradually returns. Full recovery can take 1-3 years.

Who gets frozen shoulder?

Diabetes significantly increases the risk - diabetics have 5 times higher probability of developing frozen shoulder. Other risk factors are thyroid disorders and prolonged inactivity after injury or surgery.

Prognosis: Although it takes time, 80% of patients regain good shoulder function with proper follow-up. Early treatment can reduce severity and duration.

3

Referred Pain from Neck and Thoracic Spine

Many shoulder problems are related to stiffness or dysfunction in the neck and thoracic spine. The nerves that supply the shoulder come from the neck (C4-C6), and irritation here can cause pain that feels like it comes from the shoulder itself.

Holistic Approach

That's why I always examine the neck and thoracic spine when you come with shoulder problems - not just the shoulder in isolation. Often, treating stiffness in the thoracic spine and neck gives rapid improvement in shoulder pain.

4

Red Flags

Contact emergency services immediately for:

  • Severe pain after a fall or accident, especially if you can't lift your arm
  • Shoulder that looks deformed or is "out of joint"
  • Fever together with shoulder pain
  • Numbness or tingling spreading down the arm
  • Chest pain or breathing difficulties together with shoulder pain
  • Unexplained weight loss together with persistent shoulder pain

With these symptoms - contact a doctor or emergency services, not a chiropractor first.

Book an appointment for evaluation if:

  • Pain lasting over 2-3 weeks without improvement
  • Night pain that regularly disturbs sleep
  • Gradual worsening of symptoms
  • Pain that prevents you from daily activities

How Can Chiropractic Help?

At TheBackROM I combine manual treatment with active rehabilitation. Research shows that this combination gives the best results for most shoulder problems.

Manual Treatment

I use various techniques to improve joint and muscle function:

  • Joint mobilization for better mobility
  • Soft tissue techniques for tense muscles and trigger points
  • Treatment of neck and thoracic spine - many shoulder problems are connected to stiffness here

Active Rehabilitation

Exercises are the cornerstone of effective shoulder treatment. Research shows that structured exercise programs are as effective as surgery for many conditions (Kukkonen et al., 2014). You'll receive:

  • Specific strengthening exercises tailored to your diagnosis
  • Stretches and mobilization exercises
  • Home exercises you can do between treatments
  • Guidance on ergonomics and activity modification

What Can You Expect?

First Consultation (45 minutes)

I always start with a thorough examination:

  • Discussion about your symptoms and how they affect your daily life
  • Physical examination of shoulder, neck and back
  • Testing of strength and movement
  • Clear diagnosis and treatment plan

Typical Treatment Times

  • Mild problems: 4-6 weeks
  • Moderate problems: 6-12 weeks
  • Frozen shoulder: 12-24 months (but improvement comes gradually along the way)
  • Chronic problems: May require longer follow-up

You'll see gradual improvement, not sudden cure. Most need 8-12 treatments spread over the treatment period, combined with daily home exercises.

What Can You Do Yourself?

While waiting for an appointment, or if problems are mild:

Keep Moving

Complete rest is rarely the best solution. Move your shoulder gently within what's comfortable. Avoid activities that cause severe worsening, but don't be afraid to use your arm.

Simple Exercises You Can Start With

Pendulum Exercise

Lean forward with your healthy arm supported on a table. Let the painful arm hang down. Make small circles with the arm - 10 each direction, 3-4 times daily.

Shoulder Blade Squeezes

Sit or stand straight. Pull your shoulder blades back and down toward each other. Hold 5 seconds, release. 10 repetitions, 3 times daily.

Important

Stop if exercises cause severe worsening. Some discomfort is normal, but pain that doesn't subside is a sign that you should seek help.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a referral from a doctor?

No. As a licensed chiropractor, you can come directly to me without a referral. I can examine, diagnose and treat shoulder problems. If you need further investigation or specialist treatment, I can refer you.

Do I need an MRI or X-ray first?

Usually not. A thorough clinical examination is often sufficient to make a diagnosis and start treatment. Imaging is only considered if serious injury is suspected or if symptoms don't improve after 6-12 weeks of treatment. It's important to know that findings on MRI don't always explain the pain - many completely pain-free people have rotator cuff changes on imaging.

Does insurance cover treatment?

Most private health insurance covers chiropractic treatment, typically 6-12 treatments per year. Check your own insurance agreement for details.

What if the problems come back?

40-50% experience recurrence within 1-5 years. That's why it's important to continue with exercises after the acute pain is gone. Contact us early if problems start again - this gives better results than waiting.
