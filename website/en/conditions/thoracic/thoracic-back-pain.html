Thoracic Back Pain

Complete guide to pain in the thoracic spine and upper back | Chiropractor Mads Finstad, Majorstua

The thoracic vertebrae (thoracic spine) make up the longest part of the spinal column with 12 vertebrae attached to the ribs. This part of the back is naturally stiffer than the neck and lower back, but can still cause significant discomfort.

Pain between the shoulder blades, around the rib cage or in the upper back can have many causes - from simple muscle tensions to conditions like costochondritis or rib dislocation that require specific treatment.

Quick Summary

  • 15-30% experience thoracic back pain during their lifetime
  • 80% improve with conservative treatment
  • 12 thoracic vertebrae (T1-T12) attached to the ribs
  • Most common causes: muscle tension, joint dysfunction, rib dislocation
  • Red flags: chest pain with breathing difficulties, radiation to arm

When must you seek help immediately?

In case of sudden intense chest pain with breathing difficulties, pain radiating to arm combined with nausea, or numbness/weakness in legs - call emergency services or go to the emergency room.

Diagnosis

Pain Between Shoulder Blades

The most common complaint in the thoracic area. Often caused by muscular tension, trigger points or joint dysfunction. 80% get better with conservative treatment.

Rib Dislocation

Acute pain with movement or deep breathing? Rib dislocation occurs when a rib becomes locked in the joint against the vertebra. Often dramatic pain, but good prognosis.

Read more Diagnosis

Scheuermann's Disease

Developmental disorder causing increased kyphosis (rounded back) in adolescents. Affects 4-8% of the population. Early detection is important for best outcome.

Read more Diagnosis

Flat Back (Hypokyphosis)

Too little curve in the thoracic spine can surprisingly cause more issues than rounded back. Especially affects people with rigid posture and can cause compensatory problems.

Read more Diagnosis

Costochondritis

Inflammation of the cartilage between ribs and breastbone. Often gives frightening chest pain that can be mistaken for heart disease. Common and benign condition.

Read more Diagnosis

Kyphosis (Rounded Back)

Increased curvature in the thoracic spine - from functional postural failure to structural changes. Important to distinguish between types for correct treatment.

Read more Diagnosis

Osteoporosis and Compression Fractures

Brittle bones can cause compression fractures in the vertebrae, often without obvious trauma. Especially affects women after menopause. Early prevention is important.

When should you seek help?

Acute (call 113)

  • Sudden, intense chest pain with breathing difficulties
  • Pain radiating to arm, jaw or back combined with nausea
  • Numbness or weakness in legs
  • Loss of bladder or bowel control

Within 24-48 hours

  • Fever combined with back pain
  • Pain after significant trauma
  • Significant or increasing muscle weakness

Planned assessment

  • Persistent pain affecting function
  • Numbness or tingling that does not improve
  • Pain affecting sleep and daily activities

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most common causes of thoracic back pain?
The most common causes are muscular tension (especially between shoulder blades), joint dysfunction in the thoracic vertebrae, rib dislocation, costochondritis, and posture-related problems like rounded back. In rare cases, it can differ more serious conditions like thoracic disc herniation that require investigation.
Can thoracic back pain come from the heart?
Yes, chest pain can in some cases be heart-related, especially in women where symptoms are often atypical. With sudden pain combined with breathing difficulties, nausea, radiation to arm or cold sweats you should seek emergency help.
Can a chiropractor help with thoracic back pain?
Yes, chiropractic treatment has moderate to good evidence for thoracic conditions. Manual treatment combined with targeted exercises often gives good results. Most people experience improvement within 4-6 weeks.
When should I see a doctor for thoracic back pain?
Seek medical attention for: sudden intense pain, pain radiating to the chest or arm, breathing difficulties, fever, unexplained weight loss, night pain that wakes you, or pain after trauma. Also for symptoms like numbness or weakness in the legs.
