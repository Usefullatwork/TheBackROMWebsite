Conditions I Treat Urgently on Weekends

Back Spasms and Acute Back Pain

A sudden "stab" in your back that locks you up. Back spasms are the most common reason people seek a chiropractor on weekends. Early treatment can contribute to a faster return to normal activities.

Read more about back pain

Lower Back Pain

Pain in the lower part of the back - from stiffness and aching to sharp pain with movement. About 80% of the population experiences this at least once in their lifetime. Most episodes improve significantly within 2-6 weeks.

Read more about lower back pain

Neck Stiffness and Cricks

Did you wake up with a completely stiff neck? Neck cricks often occur after sleeping in a bad position or sudden movements. These are conditions that respond well to manual treatment.

Read more about neck pain

Radiating Pain to Arm or Leg

Burning pain, numbness, or tingling that radiates down? This may indicate nerve irritation. A thorough examination will clarify whether this can be treated by a chiropractor.

Read more about nerve roots

Headache from the Neck

Headache that starts in the neck and radiates up toward the head - often on one side. Many experience relief when the underlying neck stiffness is treated.

Read more about headache

Acute Dizziness and BPPV

Did you wake up with the world spinning? BPPV (benign paroxysmal positional vertigo) is the most common cause of acute dizziness. With proper diagnosis and treatment, 74-85% become symptom-free after 1-3 treatments.

Read more about dizziness on weekends

How an Emergency Consultation Works

First Consultation (45 minutes)

I start with a thorough conversation about your symptoms. Then I perform a clinical examination to find the cause of your pain. Based on the findings, you receive treatment tailored to your condition - this may include joint manipulation, mobilization, or soft tissue treatment.

Concrete Plan

You go home with a concrete plan: what you can do yourself, what you should avoid, and whether you need follow-up. If necessary, I can refer you for imaging or to other specialists.

Follow-up Treatment (30 minutes)

Used to evaluate progress, adjust treatment, and give you tailored exercises.

4

Safe and Effective Treatment

International guidelines recommend manual treatment as one of several options for acute back and neck problems. Research shows that the effect is comparable to physiotherapy and exercise - all can be good choices depending on your preferences.

Documented Safety

A 2023 study with over 960,000 treatments showed that serious complications are very rare: only 0.21 per 100,000 treatments. The most common side effects are mild soreness or stiffness that resolves within 24-48 hours.
