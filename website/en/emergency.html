Treatment methods for acute conditions

We use effective and documented treatment methods that are particularly suitable for acute conditions:

Immediate pain relief

  • Manipulation treatment: Restores normal joint function
  • Soft tissue techniques: Reduces muscle spasms and tension
  • Dry needling: Effective against trigger points and spasms
  • Kinesiotaping: Supports injured structures

Rehabilitation and prevention

  • Exercises to prevent relapses
  • Ergonomic advice and adjustments
  • Activity modifications
  • Plan for gradual increase in activity

Follow-up after emergency treatment

After your emergency treatment, proper follow-up is important to ensure optimal recovery and prevent relapses:

First 24-48 hours

  • Follow the specific instructions you receive
  • Use ice or heat as recommended
  • Take pain medication as prescribed
  • Moderate activity - avoid complete rest
  • Call us if symptoms worsen

Further follow-up

  • Scheduled check-up within 3-7 days
  • Gradual increase in activity
  • Specific exercises for your condition
  • Preventive measures and advice
  • 24/7 phone support for questions

Frequently asked questions

How do I know if I need emergency treatment?

Call us and describe your symptoms. We assess whether the condition requires emergency treatment or if it can wait until the next business day. Generally, emergency treatment applies to intense pain that prevents normal function.

Can I get an appointment the same day?

We do our best to offer same-day appointments for truly acute conditions. Call as early as possible for best availability. Emergency appointments on weekends have limited capacity.

Does it cost more on weekends?

Yes, there is a weekend surcharge of 300 NOK for emergency treatment on Saturdays and Sundays. This covers the additional costs of keeping the clinic open outside normal opening hours.

What should I do while waiting for treatment?

Avoid activities that worsen the pain, but don't create complete bed rest. Use ice for acute inflammation or heat for muscle spasms. Take any painkillers you have at home according to normal dosage recommendations.

Can I get sick leave on weekends?

As a chiropractor, I cannot write sick leave certificates. If you need sick leave, you must contact your regular doctor or emergency services. However, I can provide a statement about your treatment if necessary.
