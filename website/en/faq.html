Important Information

Please note: The information on this website, including details on treatment, the scope of practice, insurance coverage and patient rights, is based on the healthcare system and regulations in Norway. This content is intended for patients seeking care within Norway.

General Questions

Do I need a referral from a doctor?

No. As a chiropractor, I am a primary contact in the healthcare system. This means you can come directly to me without going through your GP first.

Can a chiropractor issue sick leave?

Yes, chiropractors in Norway can issue sick leave for up to 12 weeks for conditions related to the musculoskeletal system. For disc herniations, we typically recommend 2-4 weeks for office work and 6-8 weeks for physically demanding work.

Can a chiropractor refer me to a specialist or for imaging studies?

Absolutely. If your chiropractor finds that further investigation is needed, they can refer you to specialists, such as orthopedists or neurologists. They can also order X-rays, MRI, or CT scans without going through your GP.

Does health insurance cover the treatment?

Yes, Clinic for All has agreements with most major insurance companies (Storebrand, Gjensidige, If, Tryg, etc.). Bring your case number to your first appointment, and we usually settle directly with the company.

What happened to the Helfo refund?

From January 1, 2025, the authorities reduced the refund to 4 NOK (essentially removing it) for chiropractic treatment. This means you pay the full amount yourself, unless you have health insurance through work or privately. Chiropractors retain the right to issue sick leave and refer to specialists.

Is chiropractic treatment covered by the exemption card (frikort)?

No. Chiropractic treatment is not part of the exemption card scheme. What you pay at the chiropractor does not count towards the deductible limit.

How long is a treatment session?

The first consultation lasts 45 minutes and includes conversation, examination, and treatment. Follow-up appointments are 30 minutes.

How many treatments will I need?

It varies. For acute problems, the average is 4-6 treatments. For chronic problems, it can take 8-12 treatments. We evaluate progress along the way.

What is a chiropractor?

A chiropractor is a licensed healthcare professional with a 5-6 year university education and one year of internship. Chiropractors have been authorized healthcare personnel in Norway since 1988.

What is the difference between a chiropractor and a physiotherapist?

Both are authorized healthcare personnel. Chiropractors have a 5-6 year education specializing in the spine and nervous system, and often use joint manipulation. Physiotherapists have a 3-4 year education with a broader focus on rehabilitation and exercise. Chiropractors can refer for MRI/CT and issue sick leave for up to 12 weeks.

Is it safe to go to a chiropractor?

Yes. The WHO classifies chiropractic as safe treatment. As with all treatment, temporary soreness or stiffness may occur, but serious side effects are extremely rare.

Does treatment hurt?

The goal is to relieve pain, not create it. Some techniques may feel a bit uncomfortable, but I always adjust the force to what you can tolerate. We communicate throughout.

Is it dangerous to crack the neck?

No. The sound you hear is gas bubbles being released in the joint fluid (cavitation) when pressure changes. Bones are not rubbing together. The technique is safe and effective for restoring normal movement.

What is the cracking sound?

The sound comes from gas bubbles being released in the joint fluid when the joint is mobilized. The same thing happens when you crack your knuckles. The sound is not necessary for the treatment to work.

Can a chiropractor prescribe medication?

No. Chiropractors cannot write prescriptions. If you need prescription painkillers, you must contact your GP.

Can I see a chiropractor during pregnancy?

Yes. Chiropractors use adapted techniques that are safe during pregnancy. Many pregnant women experience relief from pelvic and lower back pain. Please mention if you are pregnant when booking.

Can children and the elderly see a chiropractor?

Yes. Treatment is always adapted to age and condition. For children, softer techniques are used, and for the elderly, consideration is given to any wear and tear changes.

Do you have more questions?

Don't hesitate to contact us if you have more questions about chiropractic treatment. I am here to help you and answer anything you are wondering about.
