When should you call 113 or emergency services?

Some symptoms require immediate medical attention - not a chiropractor:

?? Call 113 or emergency services for:
  • Sudden loss of strength in arms or legs
  • Numbness around the rectum or inner thighs
  • Loss of bladder or bowel control
  • Intense pain after a fall or accident
  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing

Conditions I treat acutely

Back spasms and acute back pain

A sudden "jolt" in your back that locks you up. Back spasms are the most common reason for acute visits. Early treatment can help you return to normal activities faster.

Lower back pain

Pain in the lower back - from stiffness and aching to sharp pain with movement. About 80% of the population experiences this at least once in their lifetime.

Stiff neck and neck pain

Woke up with a completely stiff neck? This often occurs after sleeping in a bad position or sudden movements. This responds well to manual treatment.

Radiating pain to arm or leg

Burning pain, numbness or tingling radiating down? This may indicate nerve irritation. A thorough examination clarifies further treatment.

Headache from the neck

Headache that starts in the neck and radiates up to the head - often on one side. Many experience relief when the underlying neck stiffness is treated.

Acute dizziness and BPPV

Woke up with the world spinning? BPPV (crystal disease) is the most common cause of acute vertigo. 74-85% become symptom-free after 1-3 treatments.

What happens during an acute consultation

First consultation (45 minutes)

I start with a thorough conversation about your symptoms. Then I perform a clinical examination to find the cause of the pain. Based on the findings, you receive treatment tailored to your condition - this may include joint manipulation, mobilization or soft tissue treatment.

You go home with a concrete plan: what you can do yourself, what you should avoid, and whether you need follow-up. If necessary, I can refer you for imaging or to other specialists.

Follow-up treatment (30 minutes)

Used to evaluate progress, adjust treatment and give you customized exercises.

4

Safe and effective treatment

International guidelines recommend manual treatment as one of several options for acute back and neck problems. Research shows that the effect is comparable to physiotherapy and exercise - all can be good choices depending on your preferences.

? Documented safety

A 2023 study with over 960,000 treatments showed that serious complications are very rare: only 0.21 per 100,000 treatments. The most common side effects are mild soreness or stiffness that resolves within 24-48 hours.
