Chiropractic Care

Manual treatment for musculoskeletal problems | Chiropractor Mads Finstad, Majorstua

The word chiropractic comes from Greek and means "to do by hand." This describes the core of the profession - treatment is primarily manual.

Chiropractors work with diagnosis, treatment and prevention of musculoskeletal problems. When a joint doesn't move as it should, it often affects the surrounding muscles and can irritate nerves. Treatment focuses on restoring normal function through manual techniques, exercises and guidance.

Chiropractors have been licensed healthcare professionals in Norway since 1989 and received primary contact status in 2006. This means you can go directly to a chiropractor without a referral from your GP.

Quick Summary

  • What: Manual treatment of joints, muscles and nervous system
  • Status: Licensed healthcare profession since 1989, primary contact since 2006
  • Education: 5-year master's degree + 1 year internship
  • Access: Direct appointment booking - no referral needed
  • Rights: Can issue sick leave up to 12 weeks, refer to specialists and order imaging
  • First consultation: 45 minutes examination and treatment

Education and Your Rights

To become a licensed chiropractor in Norway, you need a 5-year master's degree from an ECCE-accredited institution. The education focuses heavily on anatomy, physiology, neurology and diagnostics. After the master's degree, the candidate must complete one year of internship in Norway. Then the Norwegian Directorate of Health can grant authorization.

Who Can Benefit from Chiropractic?

Chiropractic treatment is primarily aimed at musculoskeletal problems. Here are conditions where research and clinical experience show that many experience improvement:

Back Problems

Good Evidence

Acute back pain ("thrown out back," lumbago) often responds well to treatment. For chronic back pain, treatment can provide pain relief and improved function. It should often be combined with exercises and changes in activity level.

?

Neck Problems

Moderate Evidence

Stiffness, tension and "office neck" are common problems that many seek chiropractic help for. Research shows moderate evidence that manual treatment combined with exercises can be beneficial.

?

Headache

Moderate Evidence

Tension headache and cervicogenic headache (headache originating from the neck) can often be treated effectively. For migraine, the evidence is more limited, but some experience a reduction in migraine days.

?

Dizziness

Special Expertise

I have special expertise in crystal disease (BPPV) and neck-related dizziness. For BPPV, repositioning maneuvers can provide rapid improvement in 80-95% of patients.

?

Other Conditions

Broad Expertise
  • Shoulder problems (rotator cuff-related pain, frozen shoulder)
  • Elbow problems (tennis elbow, golfer's elbow)
  • Hips, knees and feet
  • Pelvic-related pain, including problems during pregnancy

When is chiropractic not suitable?

Some conditions require other forms of treatment or further investigation. I always assess whether chiropractic treatment is right for you:

  • Severe osteoporosis
  • Active infections or inflammation in the spine
  • Unstable fractures
  • Tumors in the spine
  • Serious neurological conditions

How Treatment Works

First Consultation (45 minutes)

1

Thorough Assessment

I systematically go through your medical history: When did the problems start? What triggers or relieves the pain? How does it affect your daily life?

2

Physical Examination

Observation of posture and movement patterns. I test joint mobility, examine muscle strength and check reflexes and nerve function.

3

Diagnosis and Plan

After the examination, I explain the findings in an understandable way and together we develop an individual treatment plan.

Treatment Techniques

Joint Adjustment

Precise movements to restore normal joint function. May produce a "cracking sound" - harmless and not necessary for effect.

Mobilization

A gentler technique where I move joints gradually without "cracking." Suitable for those who prefer a softer approach.

Soft Tissue Treatment

Massage, trigger point treatment and stretching to release muscle tension.

Rehabilitation

Specific exercises to strengthen weak areas and prevent relapse. Often the key to lasting improvement.

Follow-up Treatments (30 minutes)

Frequency is adapted to your condition and response. Initially, treatment 1-2 times weekly may be appropriate, then tapering as you improve. Most feel better after 3-6 treatments for acute problems. Chronic conditions may need longer follow-up.

What Does the Research Say?

Chiropractic treatment is among the best documented manual treatment forms. Here are the main findings from systematic reviews:

Lower Back Pain

Cochrane reviews show that spinal manipulation can have small to moderate short-term effect for both acute and chronic low back pain. The American College of Physicians recommends spinal manipulation as one of several non-drug first-choice treatments.

Neck Pain

Research shows that manual treatment can be beneficial for mechanical neck pain, especially when combined with exercises. The effect size is small to moderate.

Headache

For cervicogenic headache, there is moderate evidence for effect. For migraine, the evidence is more limited, with possible small reduction in number of migraine days for some.

Important to Know About Treatment Response

Treatment response varies significantly between individuals. Some experience rapid improvement, others need more time. Acute problems often respond better than chronic conditions. I continuously evaluate the effect and adjust the treatment plan as needed.

Safety and Side Effects

Chiropractic treatment performed by licensed practitioners is considered safe. The World Health Organization (WHO) and the Norwegian Knowledge Centre for the Health Services consider the treatment relatively safe when performed by a licensed chiropractor.

Common, Mild Reactions

It's normal to feel slight soreness or stiffness in the first 24-48 hours - similar to the feeling after exercise. Some may experience temporary fatigue or mild headache. Usually resolves on its own.

Serious Complications

Rare. Estimates vary from 1 per 400,000 to 1 per 2 million manipulations. Large population studies show that patients who see a chiropractor do not have higher stroke risk compared to those who see a GP.

6

When Should You Seek Other Help?

Seek medical attention or emergency services immediately for:

  • Pain combined with fever or unexplained weight loss
  • Loss of bladder or bowel control
  • Increasing paralysis or numbness in the groin area ("saddle anesthesia")
  • New pain after serious trauma or falls
  • Sudden severe dizziness, vision problems or speech difficulties

Chiropractors are trained to recognize these warning signs and will refer onward when necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a referral from a doctor?
No. Chiropractors have had primary contact status in Norway since 2006, meaning you can book an appointment directly without a referral.
How many treatments will I need?
This varies based on condition and individual factors. Many experience improvement after 3-6 treatments for acute problems. Chronic conditions may require longer follow-up. I provide an estimate after the first examination.
Is the treatment painful?
Most treatment techniques are gentle. Some may experience brief discomfort during treatment or slight soreness afterward, similar to the feeling after exercise. I always adapt the techniques to your needs.
What is the "cracking sound"?
The sound is caused by small air bubbles released from the joint fluid (cavitation). This is harmless and not necessary for treatment effect. If you prefer treatment without "cracking," alternative techniques are available.
Does insurance cover treatment?
Many private health insurances cover chiropractic treatment. Check with your insurance company. Imaging (X-ray, MRI, CT) ordered by a chiropractor is covered by public health insurance.
Do I have to continue treatment for life?
No. The treatment course has a timeframe. The goal is to solve the problem and give you tools to avoid relapse. Some choose voluntary maintenance treatment, but this is not necessary for everyone.
Can pregnant women receive chiropractic treatment?
Yes. Treatment can be safely adapted throughout pregnancy. Many seek help for pelvic and back problems during pregnancy and experience good relief.
