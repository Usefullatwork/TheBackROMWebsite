Fascial Manipulation

Targeted treatment of deep connective tissue at Klinikk for alle, Majorstua

Pain in your knee, but tests show the knee itself is healthy? Headache that starts in your neck, but neck massage only helps temporarily? Pain that feels deep and diffuse - difficult to localize precisely?

These symptoms may indicate problems in the fascia - the body's interconnected connective tissue network.

Quick Summary

  • What it is: Treatment of connective tissue (fascia)
  • Goal: Restore gliding between tissue layers
  • Suitable for: Chronic pain, referred pain
  • Treatment course: 3-6 treatments over 3-6 weeks
  • Method: Stecco method with deep friction

When Must You Seek Help Immediately?

For severe pain unlike anything previously experienced, numbness or weakness that worsens, pain with fever, loss of bladder or bowel control, or neck pain after trauma - call 113 or go to emergency room.

1

What is Fascial Manipulation?

Fascial manipulation focuses on fascia - the connective tissue that surrounds and connects all muscles, joints, nerves and organs. This isn't just "wrapping paper" for muscles, but an active system that transfers forces and coordinates movements.

Under normal conditions, the different layers of fascia glide freely against each other, lubricated by a gel-like substance called hyaluronic acid. But after injuries, overload or prolonged stress, this substance can become thicker and more "sticky" - a condition called densification.

Treatment involves deep, sustained friction directly on identified points with knuckle or elbow. This generates heat and mechanical stimulation that changes the consistency of the "sticky" substance and restores normal gliding between tissue layers.

2

The Stecco Method

Fascial manipulation according to the Stecco method was developed by Italian Luigi Stecco based on over 40 years of anatomical research. The method distinguishes between two types of points:

Centre of Coordination (CC)

Specific points where forces from multiple muscles converge. This is often where the actual cause of the problem lies. When a CC point becomes "densified," normal force transfer is disrupted.

Centre of Perception (CP)

Where you actually feel the pain - often in or around a joint. Abnormal stress from a dysfunctional CC creates pain and inflammation here over time.

To achieve lasting relief, treatment must address the cause (CC), not just the symptom (CP). This explains why massage of the painful area often only provides temporary relief.

What Can You Expect?

During Treatment

Treatment can be intense - many describe it as a "deep, burning sensation." This is brief and focused on the specific point. Many experience it as "good pain" - the feeling that the problem is finally being found.

After Treatment

Local soreness, stiffness and possible bruising is normal for 1-2 days. This shows that the planned physiological change has taken place and the body has started the healing process.

Treatment Response

Some notice improvement after the first treatment, others need more. If no improvement after 4-5 treatments, an alternative approach is considered.

What Does the Research Say?

Review of Research

Systematic Review (2021): Found low to moderate quality of available research, but positive results for pain relief and improved function.

Scoping Review (2024): Confirms the method is primarily used for chronic conditions and shows promising results. Studies on hyaluronic acid and fascia's role in pain support the theoretical background.

Conclusion: The method is built on solid anatomical knowledge and many patients experience good results. At the same time, more high-quality research is needed to establish the effect with greater certainty. I cannot promise cure - results vary between individuals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this the same as regular massage?
No, this is more specific and intense. The focus is on specific anatomical points, not general relaxation. Treatment is targeted at the cause of the problem, not just where it hurts.
How painful is it?
Treatment can be uncomfortable during the friction itself, but intensity is adapted to your tolerance. Many experience it as "good pain" - the feeling that a deep-seated problem is finally being addressed.
Can it cure my pain?
I cannot promise cure. Treatment can provide symptom relief and improved function for many, but results vary between individuals. Some experience significant improvement, others less effect.
Does insurance cover treatment?
As part of chiropractic treatment, this is often covered by private health insurance. Check your policy in advance.
Do I need a referral?
No, you can book directly. Chiropractors are primary contacts in Norway and can independently diagnose, treat and refer when needed.
How many treatments will I need?
Usually 3-6 treatments over 3-6 weeks. Some notice improvement after the first treatment, others need more. I evaluate progress and adjust the plan along the way.
