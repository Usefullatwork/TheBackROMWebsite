Graston/IASTM

Instrument-assisted treatment of muscles and tendons at chiropractic clinic Majorstua

Do you have tendon pain that won't go away? Stiff muscles that limit you? Scar tissue from an injury or surgery that feels tight?

The Graston technique, also known as IASTM (Instrument-Assisted Soft Tissue Mobilization), uses special steel instruments to treat problems in muscles, tendons and connective tissue. It makes it possible to feel and treat areas that are difficult to reach with fingers alone.

Quick Summary

  • What: Instrument-assisted soft tissue treatment with specially designed steel instruments
  • Used for: Tendon problems, stiff muscles, scar tissue and reduced mobility
  • Evidence: Moderate effect on pain and improved function (2026 study)
  • Best results: Combined with exercises and active rehabilitation
  • Course: Usually 4-6 treatments over a few weeks
1

What is Graston/IASTM?

IASTM stands for "Instrument-Assisted Soft Tissue Mobilization" - treatment of soft tissue with instruments. Graston is the most recognized and documented brand name.

The treatment is based on traditional Chinese Gua Sha, but uses modern, ergonomic instruments made of medical grade steel. The tools have different shapes and edges that make it possible to reach different body areas - from large muscle groups in the back to small structures in hands and feet.

How Does It Work?

Increased Blood Flow

Treatment stimulates local blood circulation, bringing oxygen and nutrients to the area.

Tissue Effects

Controlled friction can affect stiff and adhesive structures in connective tissue and muscle fascia.

Sensory Stimulation

Treatment affects the nervous system and can reduce pain signals from the area.

Diagnostic Tool

Instruments enhance the ability to feel abnormal structures like tight bands, scar tissue or trigger points.

2

Who Can Benefit?

Graston/IASTM is used for a range of musculoskeletal problems:

Tendon Problems (Tendinopathies)

  • Achilles tendon problems - pain behind the heel
  • Tennis elbow and golfer's elbow
  • Jumper's knee - pain below the kneecap
  • Shoulder tendon problems - rotator cuff

Foot Problems

  • Plantar fasciitis (heel pain) - research shows good effect combined with exercises
  • Stiffness after ankle injuries

Neck and Back Pain

  • Stiff neck with muscular component
  • Pain between shoulder blades
  • Low back pain with tight muscles

Scar Tissue and Stiffness

  • After surgeries
  • After major injuries
  • Improving tissue flexibility

What Does the Research Say?

Let me be honest about what we know - and don't know - about this treatment form.

The Latest Research (2026)

Moderate Effect on Pain A recent systematic review in BMC Musculoskeletal Disorders (March 2026) shows that IASTM can reduce pain - with moderate certainty in the conclusion.
Improved Function The same study shows that IASTM can improve function - but with low certainty in the conclusion.
Chronic Neck Pain For chronic neck pain and headache from the neck, research shows clear short-term effect on pain.

What Does This Mean in Practice?

Graston/IASTM appears to give better results than no treatment, especially short-term for pain. But research is also clear that best effect is achieved when instrument treatment is combined with active measures like stretching, strength exercises and movement.

This matches my own experience. I never use Graston in isolation, but as part of a comprehensive approach where I also work to strengthen and activate the area.

4

How Treatment Works

1

Examination

At first consultation, I always conduct a thorough examination with medical history, mobility tests and functional assessment. Based on this, I evaluate whether Graston technique is suitable for your specific problem.

2

Warm-up

A short warm-up may be appropriate to increase blood circulation to the area being treated.

3

Instrument Treatment

I use the instruments systematically over the relevant area. The treatment itself usually takes 30-60 seconds per area.

4

Follow-up

Afterward often follows stretching, mobilization or exercises. You get exercises to do at home to maintain the effect.

Treatment Course

Most need 4-6 treatments, usually 1-2 times per week over a few weeks. Some notice improvement after just a few treatments, while chronic problems may need longer time.

I evaluate progress along the way and adjust the plan according to how your body responds.

5

What Can You Expect?

During Treatment

You'll feel pressure and friction against the skin - some describe it as an intense massage. It shouldn't be painful, and I always adjust the pressure. Let me know if it becomes too intense.

Normal Reactions Afterward

Redness

Increased blood flow causes temporary redness that disappears within hours

Soreness

The area may feel sore for 24-48 hours, like after a hard workout

Bruising

Mild bruising may occur, especially on first treatment. Disappears within a few days

You can continue with normal activities after treatment. Ice pack can be used if the area feels warm or swollen.

6

Who Should Not Get Treatment?

Graston/IASTM isn't suitable for everyone. I always assess contraindications before treatment.

Not Appropriate For

  • Open wounds
  • Unhealed fractures
  • Active infection in the area
  • Skin conditions like eczema or psoriasis in treatment area

Caution With

  • Blood thinning medications (increased bruising risk)
  • Cancer
  • Pregnancy (certain areas)

Always tell me about medications you're taking and any health challenges.

When Should You Seek Other Help?

  • Sudden, intense pain without clear cause
  • Pain combined with fever and feeling unwell
  • Numbness or weakness that's worsening
  • Unexplained weight loss along with pain
  • Pain after accident or trauma

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Graston painful?
Treatment shouldn't hurt. You'll feel pressure and sometimes discomfort over tender areas, but I adjust the intensity. Always let me know if it becomes too much.
How many treatments will I need?
This varies. Acute problems may improve with 3-4 treatments. Chronic conditions often need 6-8 treatments or more. I assess your response along the way and give you realistic expectations.
Can I exercise afterward?
Yes, most can exercise normally. Some prefer to wait until the next day if the area is sore. Light activity the same day is often beneficial.
What's the difference between Graston and regular massage?
Graston provides more precise treatment of specific structures. The instruments help me feel and treat scar tissue, tight bands and adhesions. Massage has broader application for general relaxation.
Do bruises mean something is wrong?
No. Mild bruises are a normal reaction and mean the treatment has affected the tissue. They usually disappear within 48-72 hours and often become less noticeable with subsequent treatments.
