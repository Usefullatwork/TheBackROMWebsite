Preventive Treatment: Why Come Before It "Burns"?

Regular follow-up after you have responded well to treatment - to break the cycle of recurring pain

Your back stiffens. You seek treatment. It gets better. A few months later? The pain is back. The cycle repeats year after year. Preventive treatment (maintenance care) is about breaking this cycle before the pain takes over again.

Quick Summary

  • What: Regular follow-up after good response to treatment
  • For whom: Those with recurring back or neck pain
  • Documented effect: Approx. 13 fewer pain days per year
  • Frequency: Individually tailored, typically every 1-3 months
1

Familiar Pattern?

Your back stiffens. You seek treatment. It gets better. A few months later? The pain is back. The cycle repeats year after year.

The Cycle Many Know
Pain → Treatment → Relief → Time Passes → Pain Again
Preventive Treatment Breaks the Cycle

Or maybe it's not the pain that bothers you most, but the worry: What if it happens again? You move carefully, say no to activities, lift with fear. The uncertainty steals focus and energy.

Many people know this. Preventive treatment is about breaking the cycle.

2

What is Preventive Treatment?

Preventive treatment (often called "maintenance care") consists of regular check-ups to keep your body functioning well. Instead of waiting until the pain becomes unbearable, I work systematically to maintain what's working.

Think of it like car maintenance: You don't wait until the engine breaks down before checking the oil. With regular check-ups, I can detect small tensions and imbalances before they develop into major, painful episodes.

3

What Does Research Say?

The largest study on preventive chiropractic care comes from Scandinavia. The Nordic Maintenance Care program (published 2018) followed 328 patients with recurring low back pain over one year. All had responded well to initial treatment and were then divided into two groups.

Documented Effect

12.8 fewer pain days per year
1.7 extra consultations per year

Source: Nordic Maintenance Care program, 2018 (328 patients over one year)

This cost only 1.7 extra consultations per year. A systematic review from 2019 confirms that preventive chiropractic care can now be considered documented treatment for selected patients with recurring back problems who respond well to initial treatment.

Important Nuance

Research is clear that this is not for everyone. Preventive treatment is only recommended for patients who have had recurring episodes and who have responded well to treatment.

4

Who is it For?

Preventive treatment is relevant for you if you:

🔄 Have had multiple episodes of back or neck pain
✅ Respond well to chiropractic treatment
📅 Notice that problems return at regular intervals
💼 Have a physically demanding job or are active in sports
🎯 Want fewer pain episodes and more predictability

Are you already symptom-free after a treatment course? That's often a good time to start. It's easier to maintain good function than to restore it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to go for treatment forever?
No. The goal is to make you self-sufficient. Preventive treatment is voluntary and can be stopped at any time. Many choose to reduce frequency over time.
Is this the same as "going to the chiropractor forever"?
That's a common misconception. Preventive treatment is not something I recommend to all patients - it's for selected individuals with recurring problems who respond well to treatment. You decide if it's right for you.
Does my insurance cover this?
Most private health insurance covers chiropractic treatment, including follow-up treatments. Check the terms of your agreement. Klinikk for alle has agreements with most insurance companies.
Do I need a referral?
No, chiropractors have been primary healthcare contacts since 2006. You can book an appointment directly without a referral from a doctor.
