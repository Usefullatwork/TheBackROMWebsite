Shockwave Therapy

Modern tendon treatment at Klinikk for alle, Majorstua

Burning pain under your heel in the morning? Dull, aching shoulder pain that worsens when you lift your arm? Stabbing pain in your Achilles tendon? These problems often have something in common: the body's natural healing process has stalled. Shockwave therapy gives these areas a "kickstart" that reactivates the repair process.

Quick Summary

  • What it is: High-energy sound waves
  • Effect: "Kickstart" for healing processes
  • Treatment: 3-6 sessions of 5-10 min
  • Best documented: Plantar fasciitis, calcific shoulder
1

What is Shockwave Therapy?

Shockwave therapy uses high-energy sound waves - not electrical shocks - that are sent through the skin into damaged tissue. These are mechanical waves that work by stimulating the body's own repair mechanisms.

The treatment can be compared to jump-starting a car with a dead battery. In many chronic tendon conditions, the body's natural healing process has stalled. Shockwaves give the tissue a controlled "kickstart" that reactivates the repair process.

2

How Does It Work?

When shockwaves hit the tissue, several things happen simultaneously:

Starts Healing Process

Shockwaves create a controlled inflammatory response that signals to the body that the area needs repair.

Increases Blood Supply

Treatment stimulates formation of new blood vessels bringing fresh, oxygen-rich blood to the area.

Stimulates Tissue Production

Cells that produce collagen - the building block of tendons - are activated and begin building strong, healthy tissue.

Breaks Down Calcium

For calcific shoulder, the mechanical waves help break down calcium deposits that the body can then remove.

3

What Can Be Treated?

Conditions with Good Documentation

Good Evidence

Plantar Fasciitis (Heel Pain)

One of the best documented conditions for shockwave. Network meta-analysis from 2018 found 82.7% probability of being the most effective non-surgical treatment.

Good Evidence

Calcific Shoulder

Good effect on both pain, function and dissolution of the calcium deposits themselves. Systematic reviews show particularly good effect with high-energy treatment.

Good Evidence

Lateral Hip Pain (GTPS)

Pain on the outside of the hip responds well. Studies show that 70-80% experience good to excellent effect.

Conditions Where Treatment May Be Considered

Moderate Evidence

Achilles Pain

Works best combined with strength training. Treatment prepares the tissue, training builds the strength.

Moderate Evidence

Tennis Elbow

Newer meta-analyses from after 2019 show significant improvement in pain and grip strength. Can be considered for chronic cases.

4

Shockwave vs. Cortisone

Many face the choice between these two treatments. Research shows that shockwave therapy for plantar fasciitis provides better and more lasting pain relief than cortisone injections at 3 and 6 month follow-up.

Shockwave Cortisone
Mechanism Stimulates repair Suppresses inflammation
Effect onset Gradual over weeks Fast (days)
Duration Often long-lasting Often short-term
Safety Very safe Risk with repeated use
5

The Treatment Process

1
First Consultation

Thorough examination to assess your symptoms and determine if shockwave is right for you.

2
The Treatment

Gel applied to skin, then pressure pulses delivered to the area for 5-10 minutes. Intensity adjusted to tolerance.

3
Treatment Plan

Usually 3-6 treatments with 5-10 days between to give your body time to respond.

4
After Treatment

Mild soreness for 24-48 hours is normal. Avoid high-intensity exercise and anti-inflammatory medications.

Important After Treatment
  • Avoid anti-inflammatory medications (Ibuprofen, Diclofenac) - these can inhibit the desired repair process
  • Avoid ice on treated area
  • Avoid high-intensity exercise for 2-3 days
6

Safety and Contraindications

Shockwave therapy is very safe when performed by qualified healthcare professionals. The most common reactions are mild and temporary: soreness, redness or swelling in the treatment area. Serious complications are extremely rare in published studies.

Treatment Is Not Suitable For Everyone

Pregnancy

Treatment near the fetus is avoided

Cancer

Active cancer in treatment area

Blood Thinners

Bleeding disorders or strong blood thinners

Infection

Acute infection or open wounds in the area

Recent Cortisone

Recent cortisone injection (last 4-6 weeks)

7

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly does shockwave therapy work?
Varies greatly. Some notice relief after the first treatment, but full effect often comes weeks or months after completing the treatment series.
Can I exercise alongside treatment?
Yes, adapted exercise is crucial for lasting results. I create a tailored program that supports the healing process. Avoid high-intensity loading of the area for a couple of days after each treatment.
How many treatments will I need?
Usually 3-6 treatments with 5-10 days between. This depends on condition, how long you've had the problems and how you respond.
Is shockwave therapy painful?
You'll experience a series of rapid, intense pressure pulses that can be uncomfortable over damaged tissue, but intensity is always adjusted to what you can tolerate. Most describe it as uncomfortable but completely manageable.
Does insurance cover it?
Many private health insurances cover chiropractic treatment. Check your policy in advance.
