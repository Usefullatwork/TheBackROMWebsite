What Are Soft Tissue Techniques?

Muscle and soft tissue techniques are manual methods that focus on the body's soft structures: muscles, tendons, ligaments and connective tissue (fascia). When these structures don't function optimally, it can be due to overload, poor loading patterns or prolonged stress.

Think of the muscles as a network of elastic bands. They should glide freely over each other. When the system fails, "sticky" areas develop where the tissue doesn't glide as it should - which restricts movement and creates pain.

The goal of treatment is to reduce tension, improve movement and support the body's natural repair processes.

Treatment Techniques I Use

Muscle Energy Technique (MET)

An active technique where you use muscle force against resistance. You contract the muscle lightly for a few seconds, then relax. Then the muscle is stretched to a new end position.

Moderate to good effect for chronic neck pain and jaw pain

Post-Isometric Relaxation (PIR)

A gentler technique that uses the muscles' natural relaxation reflex. The muscle is placed in a slightly stretched position, held for a few seconds, followed by deep relaxation and improved flexibility.

Better results for neck pain than self-stretching alone

Active Release Technique (ART)

A patented technique that combines pressure with active movement. I apply pressure to tight tissue while you move your body from shortened to extended position.

Useful for pain, mobility and function in musculoskeletal problems

Trigger Point Treatment

Targeted treatment of "muscle knots" - tender points that can cause local pain and referred pain. Sustained pressure deactivates the trigger point and can reduce pain signals.

Can reduce intensity and frequency of tension headaches

Flossband (Tissue Flossing)

Strong elastic band wrapped tightly around joint or muscle group for 1-2 minutes while you perform specific movements.

Good pain relief and moderate improvement in mobility

Myofascial Release & Effleurage

Slow techniques working with the connective tissue (fascia) around all muscles. Gliding strokes improve circulation and promote relaxation.

Often used at the start and end of treatment

Each technique is chosen based on your specific condition, previous experience and preferences. Often multiple techniques are combined in the same treatment for best effect.

Treatment Course

Expected Duration

Acute problems: Often 3-6 treatments over 2-3 weeks
Chronic conditions: May require 8-12 treatments over 6-8 weeks
After treatment: Mild soreness for 1-2 days is normal

Home exercises and stretches are often an important part of treatment for lasting improvement. I evaluate progress along the way and adjust the plan as needed.

Combined Treatment

As a chiropractor, I combine soft tissue techniques with other measures when appropriate: joint treatment to improve movement, exercises to build strength, and ergonomic advice to prevent relapse.

Soft tissue techniques "open a window" where pain is reduced and movement improves. I use this window to build a stronger and more resilient body with exercises and lifestyle adjustments.

When Should You See a Doctor First?

  • Severe pain that's getting worse
  • Numbness or weakness that's increasing
  • Pain with fever
  • Large bruises or unusual bleeding
  • Sudden headache unlike anything experienced before
  • Neck pain after trauma/accident

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it hurt?
Some techniques can create temporary discomfort - often described as "good pain." Others are very mild. Treatment is always adapted to your tolerance.
How many treatments will I need?
Acute problems often respond to 3-6 treatments. Chronic conditions may need more. I evaluate progress along the way, and the goal is always to make you self-sufficient as quickly as possible.
Can I combine with other treatment?
Yes, often works best combined with exercises, ergonomic adjustments and other measures. Soft tissue techniques are rarely an isolated treatment - they're most effective as part of a comprehensive plan.
Is it safe?
For the vast majority, these techniques are safe. I always do a thorough health assessment first to identify any conditions that mean these techniques should be adapted or avoided.
Do I have to undress?
I adapt treatment to your comfort. Often practical with access to skin, but can also treat through thin clothing.
Will I get better?
Treatment response varies. Some notice immediate relief, others need more treatments. I figure out what works for you and adjust the plan along the way.
