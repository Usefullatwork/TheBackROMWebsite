Arm Pain FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about arm pain, causes and treatment

What is tennis elbow?

Overuse of tendons on the outside of the elbow. Causes pain with gripping, twisting, and lifting - especially with palm down. Name is misleading: Most get it from computer work or manual work, not tennis.

How to get rid of tennis elbow?

Over 90% improve within å year. Eccentric exercises (slow lowering of weight) are documented to be effective. Avoid activities that provoke pain, and build up gradually.

What is golfer's elbow?

Same as tennis elbow, but on the inside of the elbow. Pain with gripping and bending the wrist. Treated the same way with rest, eccentric exercises, and gradual progression.

What is carpal tunnel syndrome?

The median nerve is pinched in the wrist, causing numbness and tingling in the thumb, index, and middle fingers. Often worse at night. Affects up to 1 in 7 adults, most common in women.

Does carpal tunnel syndrome go away on its own?

Sometimes. 70% experience improvement with å night splint keeping the wrist neutral. Pregnant women often get relief after birth. For persistent complaints, surgery may be relevant.

Can arm pain come from the neck?

Yes. Irritation of nerve roots in the neck can cause radiation, numbness, or weakness in the arm - even without neck pain. That's why we always examine the neck with unclear arm pain.

Why do I get pain in my left arm?

Most common causes are muscle/tendon problems or nerve irritation from the neck. However: Pain in the left arm together with chest pain or breathing difficulties can be signs of heart problems. In doubt, call 113.

Which exercises help with arm pain?

Nerve mobilization for radiation, eccentric exercises for tendon problems in the elbow, and stretching of forearm muscles. Ergonomic adjustments at the workplace are often important.

?? RED FLAGS: When is arm pain dangerous?

Call 113 (emergency) if:

  • Pain in left arm with chest pain, breathing difficulties, nausea, or sweating (can be heart attack)
  • Sudden severe weakness in the arm without obvious cause (seek medical attention)
