Headache FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about headaches, migraines and cervicogenic headaches

What is the difference between migraine and tension headache?

Migraine: Throbbing, often one-sided, nausea, light sensitivity, worsens with activity.
Tension headache: Pressing/tight band around the head, usually both sides, rarely nausea, not worsened by light activity.

What is cervicogenic headache?

Headache stemming from the neck. Pain often starts in the neck and radiates over the head to the forehead or eye. Provoked by neck movements or sustained posture.

Can å chiropractor help with headaches?

Yes. Research shows good effect of spinal manipulation and mobilization for both cervicogenic headache and migraines. We also work with soft tissue and exercises.

Can neck problems trigger migraines?

Yes. While migraine is å neurological condition, neck tension and stiffness can act as triggers for an attack. Improving neck function can reduce frequency and intensity of attacks.

Does stress cause headaches?

Stress causes increased muscle tension (shoulders, jaw, neck), which can lead to tension headaches or trigger migraines. Stress management is often part of the treatment.

Which exercises help against headaches?

Deep neck flexor training (chin tucks), shoulder blade exercises, and stretching of the upper trapezius often help. General physical activity is also effective prevention.

How many treatments are needed?

Varies. Some feel relief after 1-2 treatments. Chronic headaches often require å course of 6-12 treatments combined with exercises to achieve lasting change.

When should I seek help for headaches?

If you take painkillers more than 1-2 times å week, if the headache affects work/daily life, or if the pattern changes.
