Jaw Pain FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about jaw pain, TMD and jaw joint disorders

What is TMD (Temporomandibular Dysfunction)?

Problems with the jaw joint and chewing muscles. Can cause pain when chewing, clicking sounds, limited ability to open the mouth, or locking. TMD is å collective term for several different conditions.

How common are jaw joint problems?

TMD affects 3-15% of the population. Most common between 20 and 45 years, and women are affected more often than men. Good news: 90-95% get better with simple treatment or on their own.

What are the symptoms of jaw joint problems?

Pain when chewing, clicking or crunching from the jaw joint, limited mouth opening, and jaw locking. Many also experience ear pain, headache in the temple, and neck tension.

Is clicking in the jaw dangerous?

No, usually not. Clicking without pain often requires no treatment. Clicking is caused by the disc in the jaw joint moving. Many live fine with clicking without it causing problems.

Can teeth grinding cause jaw pain?

Yes. Teeth grinding (bruxism) overloads the chewing muscles and the jaw joint. Often related to stress or sleep disorders. Affects 10-20% of the population.

Can stress cause jaw pain?

Yes, absolutely. Stress is one of the strongest triggers for TMD. During stress, many clench their teeth without noticing, and nighttime teeth grinding increases.

Do I need å bite splint?

Not necessarily. Bite splints can protect teeth during grinding but have limited documentation as pain treatment. For most, conservative treatment (exercises, relaxation) is the first choice.

Can å chiropractor treat jaw joint problems?

Yes. Chiropractors are qualified to treat TMD. Treatment focuses on muscles and joints in the jaw and neck. Over 95% of TMD patients also have neck problems.

Does treating the neck help when the problem is in the jaw?

Often yes. Nerve pathways from the jaw joint and upper neck meet in the same area in the brain. Research shows that neck treatment alone can significantly reduce jaw pain.

Can TMD cause ear pain?

Yes. The jaw joint lies only millimeters from the ear canal. Problems there can be felt as ear pain, tinnitus, or å feeling of fullness in the ear. Symptoms often disappear after treating the jaw.

Who treats jaw complaints - dentist or chiropractor?

Both can treat TMD. Dentists evaluate teeth and bite, and can make bite splints. Chiropractors treat muscle and joint problems in the jaw and neck. For complex problems, we collaborate.

Do I need surgery for jaw joint problems?

Rarely. Surgery is relevant for less than 5% - only after long-term conservative treatment without imprøvement and with documented structural problems. The vast majority get better without surgery.

What is å relaxed resting position for the jaw?

A simple technique: Place the tip of your tongue lightly against the roof of the mouth behind the front teeth, let the teeth be slightly apart (2-3 mm gap), and relax the chewing muscles. This breaks unconscious clenching.

When should I seek treatment for jaw pain?

If the pain lasts more than 2-3 weeks, you have trouble opening your mouth, the jaw joint locks, you have persistent headache from the jaw, or the pain affects eating or speaking.

?? RED FLAGS: When is jaw pain dangerous?

Call 113 or emergency services if:

  • Sudden locked jaw that doesn't release
  • Fever combined with jaw pain and swelling (infection)
  • Severe chest pain radiating to the jaw (heart attack)
  • Sudden facial weakness
