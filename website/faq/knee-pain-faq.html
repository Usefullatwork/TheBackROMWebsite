Knee Pain FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about knee pain, causes and treatment

What are the most common causes of knee pain?

Runner's knee (patellofemoral pain syndrome), jumper's knee (patellar tendon problems), meniscus injuries, and knee osteoarthritis. Weakness in the hip and glutes is often an underlying factor.

What is runner's knee?

Diffuse pain around or in front of the kneecap. Called patellofemoral pain syndrome. The cause often lies in weak hip and glute muscles, not the knee itself.

Do I have to stop running with runner's knee?

Not necessarily. Reduce training volume and intensity. Many can continue to run at a moderate pace. Focus on strength training for the hip and glutes in parallel.

Can I cycle with runner's knee?

Yes, often. Cycling is lower load than running. Adjust the seat so the knee isn't bent too much, and avoid high resistance. Good alternative training while you rebuild.

What is jumper's knee?

Pain in the patellar tendon, under the kneecap. Common in athletes who jump a lot. Treated with gradual load increase and eccentric exercises.

Does a meniscus injury require surgery?

Rarely for degenerative injuries (wear and tear). Research shows that exercise therapy is as effective as surgery for many. Acute injuries after twisting in younger people may require surgery.

Can the meniscus heal on its own?

Partially. The outer part of the meniscus has blood supply and can heal. The inner part has poor blood supply. Many degenerative meniscus injuries cause few problems without treatment.

What is knee osteoarthritis?

Wear and tear of the cartilage in the knee. Causes stiffness, pain with loading, and swelling. Exercise is the most important treatment - it strengthens the muscles around the knee and protects the joint.

Can I run with knee osteoarthritis?

Yes, often. Research shows that moderate running does not worsen osteoarthritis and can be beneficial. Start gradually and listen to your body. Many with mild osteoarthritis run without problems.

What exercises help knee pain?

Thigh muscle strengthening, hip muscle strengthening (important!), balance exercises, and low-impact cardio like cycling or swimming. Avoid exercises that provoke pain.

When should I seek treatment for knee pain?

If the pain lasts more than 1-2 weeks, the knee swells up, you have trouble bending or straightening the knee, or the knee gives way.

⚠️ RED FLAGS: When is knee pain dangerous?

Seek medical attention if:

  • The knee locks and cannot be straightened
  • Significant swelling after injury
  • Inability to bear weight on the knee
  • Visible deformity after injury
