Lower Back Pain FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about lower back pain, causes and treatment

What causes lower back pain?

Lower back pain can have many causes:

  • Mechanical: Muscle strain, joint dysfunction, poor posture
  • Disc-related: Disc herniation, disc degeneration, bulging disc
  • Nerve-related: Sciatica, nerve root compression
  • Structural: Arthritis, spinal stenosis, spondylolisthesis
  • Other: Stress, poor ergonomics, lack of exercise

When should I see å chiropractor for back pain?

You should seek help if:

  • Pain persists beyond å few days
  • Pain significantly limits daily activities
  • Pain radiates to the legs
  • You experience numbness or tingling
  • You have recurring episodes

?? RED FLAGS: When should I seek emergency care?

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:

  • Loss of bladder or bowel control
  • Progressive leg weakness
  • Numbness in the saddle area (groin/buttocks)
  • Severe pain following trauma
  • Unexplained weight loss or night sweats
  • Fever with back pain

What is sciatica?

Sciatica is pain that radiates along the sciatic nerve from the lower back through the buttock and down the leg. It's usually caused by:

  • Disc herniation pressing on nerve roots
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Piriformis syndrome
  • Spondylolisthesis

Symptoms include sharp, shooting pain, numbness, tingling, and weakness in the leg.

How do you treat disc herniation?

Treatment depends on severity and symptoms:

  • Initial phase: Pain relief, anti-inflammatory measures, gentle mobilization
  • Active phase: Specific exercises to centralize pain, improve movement
  • Rehabilitation: Core strengthening, flexibility, movement pattern training
  • Prevention: Ergonomic advice, lifting techniques, maintenance exercises

Most disc herniations improve with conservative treatment. Surgery is rarely needed.

Should I rest or stay active with back pain?

Stay active! Research shows that prolonged rest can delay recovery. I recommend:

  • Continue light activities within pain tolerance
  • Avoid bed rest beyond 1-2 days
  • Gradually increase activity levels
  • Use pain as å guide, not å stop sign

Will I need an MRI?

Not always. MRI is indicated if:

  • Red flag symptoms are present
  • Significant neurological deficits
  • No improvement after 6-8 weeks of conservative treatment
  • Considering surgical consultation

Many back pain cases resolve without imaging.

What are the best exercises for lower back pain?

Exercises are tailored to your specific condition, but generally include:

  • Core stability: Plank variations, dead bug, bird dog
  • Mobility: Cat-cow, child's pose, spinal rotation
  • Strengthening: Bridges, hip hinges, squats
  • Stretching: Hip flexors, hamstrings, piriformis

I'll provide å personalized exercise program during your visit.

Can chiropractic treatment help chronic back pain?

Yes, many patients with chronic back pain benefit from:

  • Manual therapy to improve joint mobility
  • Soft tissue techniques for muscle tension
  • Exercise rehabilitation to build resilience
  • Pain science education
  • Lifestyle modifications

Is it safe to get adjusted if I have å disc herniation?

Yes, when done correctly. I use gentle, specific techniques appropriate for disc problems. Treatment is modified based on your symptoms and examination findings.

How can I prevent lower back pain?

Prevention strategies include:

  • Regular exercise: Maintain strength and flexibility
  • Good ergonomics: Proper workstation setup, lifting techniques
  • Weight management: Maintain healthy body weight
  • Stress management: Tension contributes to pain
  • Movement variety: Avoid prolonged static positions

What about sleeping positions with back pain?

Recommended sleeping positions:

  • Side sleeping: Pillow between knees to align spine
  • Back sleeping: Pillow under knees to reduce lumbar curve
  • Avoid stomach sleeping: Can strain the lower back

A supportive mattress is also important.

Does back pain always show on imaging?

No. Many people have imaging findings (disc bulges, arthritis) without pain, and many with pain have normal imaging. Pain is complex and involves more than just structural changes.

Can stress cause back pain?

Yes, absolutely. Stress can:

  • Increase muscle tension
  • Alter movement patterns
  • Amplify pain perception
  • Reduce pain tolerance

Addressing stress through relaxation techniques, exercise, and lifestyle changes is often part of treatment.

Will my back pain come back?

Some people have recurring episodes. To reduce risk:

  • Complete your rehabilitation program
  • Continue maintenance exercises
  • Practice good ergonomics
  • Address contributing factors (stress, weight, fitness)
  • Consider periodic maintenance visits

How long will recovery take?

Recovery time varies:

  • Acute strain: 1-3 weeks
  • Disc herniation with sciatica: 6-12 weeks
  • Chronic pain: Several months of rehabilitation

Most people see significant improvement within the first few weeks of treatment.
