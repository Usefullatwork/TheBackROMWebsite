Shoulder Pain FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about shoulder pain, causes and treatment

What are the most common causes of shoulder pain?

Rotator cuff problems (injury or inflammation in shoulder muscles), tendon impingement, and frozen shoulder (stiffness). Shoulder pain can also come from the neck.

What is shoulder impingement?

Tendons get pinched between the shoulder roof and upper arm bone. Causes pain when lifting arm above head and often night pain. Recent research calls this rotator cuff-related shoulder pain.

What is frozen shoulder?

Gradually increasing stiffness and pain in the shoulder joint that goes through three phases: pain-dominated (3-9 months), stiffness-dominated (4-12 months), and thawing phase (5-26 months). Most common between 40-60 years. Diabetics have 4 times higher risk.

How long does frozen shoulder last?

Typically 1-2 years, sometimes up to 3 years. Treatment can relieve pain and improve function along the way, even if the timeline doesn't change dramatically.

Does shoulder pain go away on its own?

Often, but it takes time. Around 50% improve within 18 months without special treatment. Treatment can shorten the pain period and improve function faster.

Why does the shoulder hurt at night?

Night pain is common with rotator cuff problems and frozen shoulder. Lying position increases pressure on structures that are already irritated. Try sleeping on the healthy side with å pillow supporting the painful arm.

Can I exercise with shoulder pain?

Yes, but modify training. Avoid lifting above head and movements that provoke pain. Focus on exercises below the pain threshold. Strengthening the rotator cuff is often useful.

Which exercises help with shoulder pain?

Pendulum exercises, shoulder blade exercises, rotator cuff strengthening, and stretching. Start gently and increase gradually. I can show you exercises adapted to your condition.

When should I seek treatment for shoulder pain?

If pain lasts more than 1-2 weeks, you have night pain disturbing sleep, pain affects daily activities, or you experience weakness or numbness in the arm.

?? RED FLAGS: When is shoulder pain dangerous?

Seek medical attention if:

  • Severe pain after injury with inability to move shoulder (can be dislocation)
  • Pain in left shoulder with chest pain and breathing difficulties (can be heart problems)
